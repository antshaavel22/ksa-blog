The discipline of refractive surgery has progressed through three primary generations of methodology: surface ablation (such as TransPRK, implemented commercially as Flow3), lamellar ablation involving a flap (LASIK), and small incision lenticule extraction (SMILE)
. In clinical practice, marketing promises of rapid recovery often dominate the conversation, overshadowing essential facts regarding long-term structural integrity and objective visual quality. Based on data spanning more than 55,000 touch-free and cut-free laser procedures performed at KSA Silmakeskus, this review analyses objective clinical metrics, corneal biomechanics, and the authentic, biologically grounded recovery timeline
.
Surgical Intervention and the Initial Post-Operative Days Across all modern methods, the patient's time in the operating theatre is brief, averaging only 5 to 7 minutes per eye
. With Flow3, the excimer laser operates exclusively on the corneal surface for just 10 to 30 seconds
. Unlike LASIK and SMILE, which necessitate a physical tissue incision and deeper manipulation of the corneal stroma, Flow3 is completely touch-free and cut-free
. It remains the only procedure where patients perceive immediate objective visual improvement, allowing them to leave the clinic 15 minutes later seeing significantly better than when they arrived
.
However, authentic physiological healing demands patience during the first week. On the first day, vision is relatively clear, and the eyes feel comfortable
. On the second and third post-operative days, patients encounter genuine physical discomfort: the eyes water, itch, and produce a persistent sensation comparable to chopping onions
. This is a biological necessity as the superficial layer (the epithelium) regenerates from the periphery towards the centre
. By the fifth or sixth day, once epithelial regeneration is complete, the protective lenses are removed, discomfort ceases instantaneously, and visual acuity typically reaches 0.8 or better
. By day seven, all physical restrictions are lifted, permitting a return to contact sports and swimming—activities strictly prohibited for weeks following LASIK and SMILE
.
Long-Term Visual Quality and the 2025 Optical Aberration Data Visual rehabilitation continues steadily between the second and fifth weeks, with 95% of patients achieving 20/20 visual acuity
. Yet, the most profound changes occur between the third and sixth months as the cornea remodels and severed nerves reconnect
.
The most recent optical aberration data from a 2025 study in BMC Ophthalmology further clarifies the clinical quality of these methods
. At six months post-operatively, patients in the TransPRK (Flow3) cohort demonstrated the lowest total higher-order aberrations (HOA) at 0.25 ± 0.07 µm, which was significantly lower than the SMILE cohort at 0.30 ± 0.08 µm and the FS-LASIK cohort at 0.38 ± 0.09 µm
. Furthermore, spherical aberrations were lowest for TransPRK (+0.05 µm) when compared to both SMILE (+0.08 µm) and LASIK (+0.15 µm)
. Coma was also lowest in the TransPRK group (0.12 ± 0.04 µm) relative to FS-LASIK (0.22 ± 0.06 µm)
.
Night Vision Comparison: Flow3 vs SMILE These exact measurements heavily influence night vision and contrast sensitivity. When comparing Flow3 and SMILE in dim light or dark conditions (3 cd/m²), TransPRK recorded significantly higher contrast sensitivity across all spatial frequencies (3, 6, 12, 18 cpd)
. A primary factor is that the larger optical treatment zones of Flow3 effectively accommodate naturally dilated pupils in darkness, mitigating aberration spread
. In SMILE surgery, the extraction of the lenticule can result in slight stromal irregularities, which induces slightly higher higher-order aberrations in dark vision than Flow3
.
Corneal Biomechanics: How Flow3 Maintains Zero Ectasia over 21 Years A central question often raised in the literature is how Flow3 maintains a zero-ectasia rate across 21 years and more than 55,000 procedures
. The explanation lies directly in corneal biomechanics. In medical literature, the incidence of ectasia following LASIK is roughly 90 cases per 100,000 procedures, and for SMILE it is about 11 cases
. Published in silico and ex vivo studies demonstrate that LASIK flap creation permanently reduces the tensile strength of the cornea, with reports indicating a regain of only 2.4% to 28.1% of the original strength at the flap interface
. Surface ablation intrinsically preserves the structural continuity of the cornea, as no flap is created and no deep intrastromal manipulation occurs, practically eliminating the biomechanical risk factors for ectasia
.
In conclusion: LASIK offers its absolute best visual experience on the second post-operative day, but at the hidden cost of a potentially lifelong flap and higher long-term complication risks
. The Flow3 method demands physical patience from the patient during the first week, but through its cut-free methodology, it secures uncompromised biomechanical integrity and exceptional visual quality for the rest of the patient's life
.
