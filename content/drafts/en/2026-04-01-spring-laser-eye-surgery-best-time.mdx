We've written before about why different seasons—summer, autumn, winter—each work well for eye surgery. The truth is, there's no single 'best' time. Much depends on your personal preferences and lifestyle. But spring? Spring has some genuinely strong advantages worth considering.

## You'll Actually Be Outside

Warmer weather means more time in fresh air. And that matters. Fresh air increases oxygen intake, which supports better recovery and helps you feel genuinely rested. Rest is critical in the first weeks after [Flow3 laser eye surgery](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/). Your first week involves strict limits: no computer work, no gym, no sauna, no intense activity. But walking outdoors? Absolutely recommended. Just remember: spring sun gets stronger. UV protection is essential—wear sunglasses even on cloudy days during recovery.

## Spring Is When People Actually Take Time Off

Spring and early summer are prime holiday season. If other times of year feel packed (winter holidays with family, summer trips abroad), spring offers breathing room. Your schedule is looser. Suddenly, setting aside recovery time feels manageable, not impossible.

## Restrictions Create Unexpected Opportunity

When travel feels complicated, people think differently about priorities. Many of our patients have told us the same thing: investment restrictions made them finally invest in something that lasts—vision correction. No airfare. No hotels. Just one procedure that changes your life.

## Summer Vision Is Fully Restored

If spring feels calm, summer is when life accelerates. Travel, sport, outdoor hobbies—it all happens in summer. Do your procedure in spring, and by summer your eyes are healed. No glasses. No contact lenses. Just clear vision for beach days, active sports, hiking, diving, whatever comes next.
