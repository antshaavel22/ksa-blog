## Three times's the charm

Kristin describes herself as an active mother, a grounded person with a passion for healthy living and content creation. Her story of finally breaking free from glasses stands out for one simple reason: it took three attempts before she was ready to commit.

It wasn't hesitation or fear that delayed her. It was circumstances — and eventually, the right clinic at the right time.

## A lifetime in frames

Kristin has worn glasses since second grade. What began as uncomfortable plastic frames gradually became just part of who she was. She tried contact lenses later, but they irritated her eyes relentlessly, so she went back to specs.

Over the years, though, glasses became more than an inconvenience. They got in the way — especially during workouts. The turning point came in a maternity ward: due to severe myopia, Kristin was terrified to remove her glasses because she couldn't see her newborn without them. She spent those precious first moments squinting through lenses.

That moment crystallized something. She didn't want to live like this anymore.

## Two setbacks, then success

For years, the dream of ditching glasses sat quietly in the back of her mind. When she finally decided to act, her first attempts in Latvia went nowhere — the clinic there couldn't perform the procedure on her.

She then turned to Estonia and found [KSA Silmakeskus](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/). The first visit was straightforward: a thorough eye examination revealed no serious contraindications, just mild inflammation in one eye. Kristin went home, received treatment at the University of Tartu clinic, and waited.

On the third attempt — back at KSA — everything checked out. The green light was finally on.

## Recovery that surprised her

Several friends had warned Kristin about a grueling recovery period. Their horror stories turned out to be overblown. Within a couple of days, she slipped back into her normal rhythm.

The initial slight blurriness faded quickly. Consistent eye lubrication kept the healing process comfortable. One thing she emphasizes: sunglasses afterward are non-negotiable. The eyes need protection during those early weeks.

The [Flow3 procedure](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) — a flapless, surface-based laser correction — meant less invasive surgery and faster healing. For someone who wanted to get back to training, that mattered.

## The freedom she'd been chasing

After the procedure, everything shifted. Kristin could run without glasses sliding down. She could hit the gym without them fogging up or slipping during burpees. She could see clearly in the rain, at dawn, on the trail.

That freedom wasn't just physical. It was psychological too.

She threw herself into fitness with renewed passion and eventually qualified as a personal trainer. Today, she works in that field full-time — something that would have been impossible with the constant friction of glasses.

She can wake up in the morning and see her children's faces before reaching for anything on the nightstand. No more squinting. No more searching. Just clear sight.

## A simple dream, finally real

Kristin's life hasn't changed dramatically in outline. She still values time in nature, good food, and family. But the texture of those moments is richer now. Everything is in focus — literally and figuratively.

What once felt impossible — waking up and seeing without aid — is now ordinary. And that ordinariness, she says, is everything.
