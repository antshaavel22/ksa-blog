## 15 of the Most Common Questions About Laser Eye Surgery

Thinking about laser eye surgery but still have doubts? You're not alone. We've compiled the 15 questions our patients ask most often before making their decision. By the end of this article, you'll have a clear picture of how [Flow3 works](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/), what to expect, and whether it's the right choice for you.

## 1. What exactly is Flow3 laser eye surgery?

Flow3 is a flapless, bladeless laser procedure that reshapes your cornea using the world's most advanced SCHWIND Amaris 1050RS laser. It corrects myopia (nearsightedness) and eliminates the need for glasses or contact lenses.

## 2. Who is suitable for Flow3?

You're a good candidate if:

- You're between 18 and 45 years old
- Your vision has been stable for the past year
- Your eyes are healthy, with no serious eye conditions
- You want to stop wearing glasses or contacts

A pre-procedure eye examination and consultation at KSA Silmakeskus will confirm your suitability.

## 3. Does the procedure hurt?

No. Flow3 is completely painless. We use advanced numbing eye drops to ensure comfort throughout. According to patient feedback, the most unusual sensation is actually the cool anesthetic drop running down your cheek.

## 4. How long does the procedure take?

The laser itself works for just 30 seconds per eye. The entire procedure takes 10–15 minutes, including preparation. You'll spend roughly an hour at the clinic in total, during which we do a final check of your correction, help you choose sunglasses, and offer you tea, coffee, or a smoothie while you relax.

## 5. Will my vision be perfect after surgery?

Immediately after the procedure, your vision will be slightly blurry, and we don't recommend driving. Most patients achieve the same clarity they had with glasses within a few days to a week. Maximum visual sharpness (100% or 20/20 vision) develops gradually during the healing process.

## 6. How long is the recovery period?

Most patients notice significant improvement by day three. Full recovery usually takes about 7 days, during which you'll use prescribed eye drops. We recommend avoiding bright light, swimming, and intense exercise during this time.

## 7. Can I go back to work the next day?

We suggest avoiding screens for the first 3–4 days, though many patients prefer to take a full week off. We recommend resting, taking it easy, and spending time outdoors when possible—all of which supports faster healing.

## 8. When can I start exercising again?

Light activities like walking or yoga are fine within a few days. After one week, when your protective contact lenses are removed, all lifestyle restrictions end. Most patients can return to their normal training routine, including intensive workouts, swimming, and contact sports.

## 9. What are the risks?

Flow3 is among the safest refractive procedures available. Over 55,000 procedures have been performed successfully at KSA Silmakeskus. Serious complications are extremely rare. During your consultation, we'll discuss individual risk factors and what to expect during healing.

## 10. Will I need glasses again in the future?

Flow3 provides permanent vision correction. However, after age 40–45, presbyopia (age-related loss of close-up focus) naturally occurs in all eyes—this isn't a complication but a normal part of aging. Some patients opt for [ICB lens replacement](https://ksa.ee/en/icb-time) instead, which addresses this in advance.

## 11. How much does Flow3 cost?

Pricing depends on your prescription strength and individual needs. Check our [detailed pricing information](https://ksa.ee/en/price-list/) or book a consultation to receive a personalized quote.

## 12. What's the difference between Flow3 and other laser procedures?

Unlike traditional LASIK (which creates a flap in your cornea), Flow3 is flapless. This means:

- Stronger corneal integrity
- Better for contact sports and high-impact activities
- Faster healing
- Lower risk of dry eye complications

Our clinical team chose Flow3 for their own eyes—that's the ultimate endorsement.

## 13. Do I need to stop wearing contacts before the procedure?

Yes. We recommend stopping contact lens use for 2–3 weeks before your consultation and pre-procedure assessment. This allows your cornea to return to its natural shape, ensuring accurate measurements.

## 14. Can both eyes be treated on the same day?

Yes. We typically treat both eyes during a single visit, one after the other. The procedure takes only 10–15 minutes total.
