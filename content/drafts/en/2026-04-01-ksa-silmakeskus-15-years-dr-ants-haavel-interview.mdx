## How KSA Silmakeskus Started: The Origin Story

In 2004, Dr Ants Haavel had a clear vision: build a clinic in Tallinn that would offer world-class laser eye surgery and advanced diagnostic imaging. By January 2005, the right building appeared at the corner of Raua and Vilmsi streets. After renovation and design work, the clinic opened its doors on 11 May—with the first patient walking through them that same day.

From the start, KSA invested in the best technology available. The Carl Zeiss Meditec MEL 80 laser was paired with top-tier diagnostic equipment. In 2005, KSA was likely one of Estonia's best-equipped private eye clinics. Colleagues from across Europe and beyond—especially the USA and India—generously shared their expertise during those early years. The timing was right. Trust came quickly.

By 2008, KSA had become one of the region's largest providers of laser correction for myopia. The growth was organic, but it was fast.

## Then and Now: What Changed

There's a lesson in how KSA started slowly. On day one, we didn't have a queue. That gave us space to learn, to refine, to get things right. Most of our patients came—and still come—by referral. That tells you something about what we deliver.

Within a year, we were at full capacity. Early on, we offered various laser and radiofrequency procedures. But I learned something fundamental: if you want to be the best, you focus. You master every detail of what you do. You don't spread yourself thin.

I also realised that staff training wasn't a cost—it was the foundation of everything. We invested a significant portion of our profits into continuous education. Many of our team have attended conferences, training programmes, and visited clinics across the world. From India to Lisbon, from learning to teaching—that culture of excellence matters.
