For severe acne, isotretinoin (Accutane, Absorica) is one of the most effective drugs we have. The skin clears up. The complication is small and quiet: somewhere along the way, your eyes start to feel dry, red and gritty, and most patients have no idea why.

The why is simple, and worth knowing if you are also thinking about laser eye surgery.

## What isotretinoin does to the eye

Isotretinoin works by reducing oil production in the skin. The body, unfortunately, is connected. The same drug shrinks the meibomian glands inside the eyelids, which produce the oily layer of your tear film. Without that oily layer, tears evaporate almost instantly and the eye surface is left exposed.

The result is significant dry eye, a feeling that something is stuck in the eye, and inflammation of the eye surface and eyelids (a condition called keratitis). Many patients also become unusually sensitive to bright light and notice their night vision is not quite what it was.

## Why this matters before laser surgery

Refractive surgery depends on a stable, healthy tear film for two reasons. First, the measurements we take of your eye before surgery are done off the tear film; if the film is unstable, the readings are smudged. Second, recovery after the procedure depends on a moist eye surface to heal smoothly. Both go wrong on isotretinoin.

For this reason, isotretinoin is treated as a temporary contraindication. The standard rule at quality clinics: stop the medication at least two weeks before surgery, and avoid it for at least four weeks afterwards. This is something to coordinate with your dermatologist, not decide on your own.

## A simple plan

**1. Tell us.** If you have taken isotretinoin recently, or are still on it, the eye specialist needs to know. We plan around it.

**2. Time it right.** With your dermatologist, plan a pause around your surgery date. Do not stop on your own without that conversation.

**3. Hydrate the surface.** While you are on the medication, preservative-free artificial tears or hydrating ointments make the day-to-day much more comfortable.
