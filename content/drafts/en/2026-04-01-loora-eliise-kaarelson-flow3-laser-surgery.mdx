Loora-Eliise Kaarelson is a singer who lights up stages and screens. She's appeared in the TV series *Reetur* and the film *Tõde ja õigus*. Her voice reached millions through *Estonia's Got Talent*. Every week, she performs across Estonia with her duo, Loora & Kauro.

Behind all these achievements lay one invisible obstacle: severe myopia (–5 diopters) that made her already packed schedule even more challenging.

## Contact Lenses Were Exhausting Her Eyes

For over half her life, Loora-Eliise wore glasses. With her prescription, contact lenses became a necessity—both on stage and off. At first, they felt convenient. But over time, the daily grind wore on her eyes. During intensive touring seasons, when performances back-to-back left no time for recovery, her eyes would dry out and fatigue would set in.

For a performer who needs to shine under lights and connect freely with an audience, this discomfort is a genuine problem.

She recalls one winter day between performances when she tried giving her eyes a break from lenses. She stepped from the frozen air into a warm room, and her glasses instantly fogged—then cracked straight down the middle. The next two weeks, she had no choice but to wear only contacts while waiting for new frames.

## The Solution That Changed Everything

Loora-Eliise had dreamed of laser eye surgery for years. But her prescription kept shifting—until spring this year. Her latest examination showed her vision had finally stabilized. She could have the [Flow3 procedure](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) performed right away.

The decision was simple. She wanted clear eyes before summer arrived, ready to face every performance and adventure without compromise. "Will I actually be able to swim this summer?" she thought. "Without that blurry haze in front of my eyes?"

## Recovery Faster Than Expected

Loora-Eliise had prepared for a full two weeks of recovery. What surprised her was how quickly her eyes healed. By the fourth day, her vision was already clear enough for daily life.

Flow3 works differently from traditional LASIK. There's no flap created—no cut into the cornea. Instead, the laser reshapes the surface layer directly, which means faster healing and a stronger cornea left intact. This makes it the ideal choice for active people: athletes, performers, anyone who can't afford extended downtime.

Within a week, Loora-Eliise was back to her normal rhythm. No contacts. No glasses fogging up in temperature changes. No dry, tired eyes after a long day on stage.

## Life on Her Terms Now

For the first time in decades, Loora-Eliise experiences the world without visual correction—without the daily thought of "Will my lenses be comfortable today?" or "Do I have backup glasses?"

She swims freely. She performs under stage lights without worrying about contacts drying out. She moves through her days with one less thing to manage.

"Choosing Flow3 was one of the best decisions I've ever made," she reflects. "It gave me my freedom back."
