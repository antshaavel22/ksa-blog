## Oskar: Actor, Adventurer, Now Perfectly Clear Vision

Oskar performs at Viljandi Ugala theatre. He snowboards, hikes, solves crosswords, bakes bread, cycles, plays table tennis with friends, and walks his dog. Life is full of small joys—and now that his vision is perfect, he's enjoying it even more.

But it wasn't always this way.

## Fifteen Years of Compromise

Oskar got his first pair of glasses in school when the blackboard blurred from the back of the classroom. He was an active footballer, so contact lenses followed soon after. That decision meant 15 years of small frustrations: solution bottles, backup pairs everywhere, and the sudden panic when a lens slipped out during rehearsal with no spare in sight.

Over time, it stopped feeling like a choice and started feeling like a chore.

## Why Laser Surgery?

The idea of fixing his vision had lingered for years. When Oskar turned 30, the question became impossible to ignore: why spend more decades dependent on lenses and glasses when he could see clearly without them?

"If you add up all the costs, maybe I'll actually come out ahead living with perfect vision," he jokes. "And at least I won't leave this world with contact lenses in my eyes."

When friends recommended [KSA Silmakeskus](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) with genuine enthusiasm, the decision became clear.

## Recovery: Better Than Expected

The procedure day brought mild discomfort. By the next morning, Oskar realised everything was progressing faster than he'd feared. Friends had warned him recovery could be difficult. The opposite happened: each day was better than the last.

He returned to theatre rehearsals two days later. Three weeks after surgery, he was back on stage performing in front of an audience—with vision sharper than ever, just moving more cautiously around the bright stage lights.

## Life Gets Clearer

Everything became more detailed, more vivid. The view from his home window across Lake Viljandi looked even more beautiful. Theatre performances were suddenly crisp and clear from every seat.

One small pleasure he now enjoys: spotting a friend across the street and waving first, knowing he actually recognises them. If it turns out to be a stranger? At least he can't blame his eyesight anymore.

## What's Next?

Oskar admits he was once careless with contacts—wearing them too long, even swimming in them. Now he has a new goal: exploring the underwater world properly. With perfect vision and healthy eyes, he's finally ready.

If you've spent years managing glasses and contacts, it might be time to ask yourself the same question Oskar did: why wait?
