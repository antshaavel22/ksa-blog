## Polina's Story: Why She Chose Laser Eye Surgery

Polina works at KSA Silmakeskus as an optometrist assistant—the kind of person who watches patients regain clear vision every single day and genuinely loves it. She's drawn to the moment when someone opens their eyes after a procedure and realises the world is sharp again. But before that, she lived that frustration herself.

Outside work, she's a woman in motion: swimming, hiking, ceramics, and dog training for shows. That last one demands precision, focus, and commitment. All things that don't pair well with glasses slipping down your nose.

### Fifteen Years in Frames

Polina got her first glasses at 15. Contact lenses? She tried them, mostly for sports or nights out. But they came with their own headaches.

"Contact lenses made my eyes dry. Every morning was a battle—fumbling to get them in properly while still half asleep."

As for the glasses themselves:

"When you wear the same frames every day, they stop being invisible. You want new ones. Then you want different ones. But good frames are expensive."

One holiday, the real cost of glasses hit home. She left them on a shelf. A child broke them in half. For five days—stuck on holiday, unable to see faces clearly, missing the moment while living through photos later.

### The Decision

Here's the thing about working at an eye clinic: you see the proof.

Most of KSA's team has already had [Flow3 laser eye surgery](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/). Not because they had to. Because they chose it for themselves. When you're surrounded by colleagues who trusted their own vision to a procedure, the decision becomes simple.

"When the opportunity came up, I didn't overthink it. I booked a slot."

### Recovery: What Actually Happens

The first few days surprised her—but pleasantly.

Before surgery, her distance vision was blurry, near vision sharp. After? Flipped. For four days, she could see far away crystal-clear, but reading was fuzzy.

"Then on day five, both came into focus. My eyes were balanced again."

The recovery itself was quiet. Long walks. Household tasks. Minimal screen time—which turned out to be exactly what she needed.

"Honestly? The digital detox did me good. I wasn't tempted to stare at a phone or computer for hours."

One habit lingered: reaching for glasses that weren't there, even at bedtime. The muscle memory of 15 years doesn't fade in a week.

### Freedom in Water

Now, months later, the biggest win is simple: swimming.

"I go to the pool without a second thought. No glasses to remove. No contacts drying out. I can dive, stay in the sauna, do what I want—and actually see while doing it."

For someone whose life includes active pursuits and time around water, [Flow3](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) wasn't just a nice idea. It was the obvious choice.

Dr. Ants Haavel and the team at KSA have performed over 55,000 procedures. Polina is one of them—and now, every patient she helps prepare for their own surgery gets to hear her real story, unfiltered. That might be worth more than any brochure.
