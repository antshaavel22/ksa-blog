## The 15-Year Battle with Glasses and Lenses

Agnes is Head of Sales at KSA Silmakeskus, where she spends her days building teams and driving growth. Away from the office, she's equally driven—HIIT workouts, active travel, the kind of person who can't sit still. But for 15 years, since age 12, she couldn't see without help. Glasses first. Then contact lenses. Then both.

Contact lenses became routine, yes. But routine isn't the same as convenient.

"I was constantly thinking about logistics," Agnes recalls. "Do I have a spare pair? Solutions? A case? What happens if I lose one?" The worst was the half-blind days—like a trip to Mexico when her lenses ran out and the local pharmacy could only offer weaker prescriptions. "I was practically half-blind for half the holiday," she says.

Then there were the small humiliations. Walking around with one lens missing. Waking up unsure if you'd remembered to take them out. A spontaneous nap that required planning.

## Why Now?

When you work at an eye clinic, the answer to your vision problem isn't far away. But the real motivator wasn't convenience—it was freedom.

"I wanted to enjoy sports and travel without constant planning," Agnes explains. "I decided I was done worrying about whether I had everything I needed to see the world clearly."

She chose [Flow3](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/), KSA's flapless surface laser procedure. No blade. No flap. Just precision.

## One Week to Perfect Vision

Some patients report longer recoveries. Agnes wasn't one of them.

"One week after the procedure, I was seeing 100% clarity with no discomfort, no pain—nothing. A couple of days of slight blurriness up close, that's all." She took the first week easy (no work, no intense exercise), but life carried on: walks, cooking, time with people she loved. Within days, normal life resumed.

## The Freedom Afterwards

The real change isn't measured in vision tests. It's in the small moments.

"It's so nice to go to bed without wondering if I actually took my lenses out," Agnes says. "Now I can have a spontaneous nap whenever I want. That used to be nearly impossible."

For someone who values independence and freedom—who won't let anything slow her down—this was the right decision.
