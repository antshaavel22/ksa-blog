## The Remarkable Health Benefits of Mushrooms

**Original source: Lindsay Oberst, Food Revolution Network**

You've probably heard mushrooms are good for you. In fact, they're so beneficial that people often use them as medicine. You might also know that virtually every ancient civilisation across the globe used mushrooms for thousands of years as healing agents. The ancient Egyptians even called them "the plant of immortality." But you may not realise just how dramatically edible mushrooms can transform both human health and the health of our planet.

## Mushrooms: A Powerhouse for Human Wellness

Here's something that might surprise you: humans are more closely related to fungi than to any other kingdom of life. Some of the fundamental molecules in mushrooms have been so present in our diet for so long that our bodies have become dependent on them—which may partly explain why mushrooms are so extraordinarily beneficial to us.

Mushrooms are nutrient-dense and among the most health-promoting foods on Earth.

Approximately 50% of edible mushrooms are classified as **functional foods**, meaning they offer nutritional value *plus* potential positive health effects beyond basic nutrition.

Countless scientific studies have revealed numerous ways mushrooms can help prevent and treat serious health challenges, while boosting overall wellness. Research shows that mushroom consumption may benefit more than **200 different health conditions**, with mushrooms delivering over 100 beneficial effects to the body.

## Nutritional Profile: What Makes Mushrooms Special

Mushrooms pack impressive nutritional density into a low-calorie package. They're excellent sources of fibre and protein. Beyond that, they contain numerous beneficial nutrients including B vitamins, selenium, potassium, copper, and—particularly when grown in sunlight—**vitamin D**. Despite their typically pale appearance, white mushrooms contain **as many antioxidants as more colourful fruits and vegetables**.

## Strengthening Immunity Through Mushrooms

Research consistently shows that eating mushrooms can have significant immune-boosting effects. A clinical study conducted by the University of Florida's Department of Food Science and Human Nutrition found that daily consumption of shiitake mushrooms enhances immune function in measurable ways.

The compounds in mushrooms—particularly beta-glucans and polysaccharides—activate immune cells and support the body's natural defence mechanisms. Regular mushroom consumption may help your body fight infection and maintain optimal wellness.
