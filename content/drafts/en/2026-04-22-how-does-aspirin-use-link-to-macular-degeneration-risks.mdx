Research has found a direct connection between how often you take aspirin and your risk for developing age-related macular degeneration (AMD). Specifically, a 2012 study revealed that frequent use of aspirin is linked to a higher risk of developing both early AMD and the more severe wet late AMD
.
The study also highlighted that this risk is tied to the amount of aspirin you take, meaning the more aspirin you consume over time, the higher your risk becomes for these macular degeneration conditions
.
While aspirin generally has very few direct side effects on the eyes compared to other medications
, it is also interesting to note that aspirin can actually be secreted into your tears
. When this happens, it can form tiny crystals in the tear film, which is another reason it might contribute to eye irritation or dryness
, even though it is less likely to cause dry eye than other common pain relievers like ibuprofen
.
If you take aspirin regularly for your heart or for pain management, you should never stop taking it on your own
. Instead, it is always a great idea to discuss your routine medications with your doctor and eye specialist so they can help you safely manage your overall health while keeping your eyes in brilliant shape!
