## Where Glasses and Contact Lenses Become Real Problems

If you're not an elite athlete, you might think: "So what's the difference?" But imagine your favourite sport:

- **Paddle and tennis** — glasses slip down mid-swing, the ball blurs, your peripheral vision narrows
- **Triathlon** — you can't see underwater without lenses, and swapping glasses between swimming and cycling is its own nightmare
- **The gym** — glasses fog up and slide with sweat, contacts dry out under the air conditioning
- **Swimming** — wearing lenses means a sharp spike in eye infection risk. Your glasses stay in the locker, useless

- **Judo, wrestling, MMA** — in contact sports, glasses are nearly useless and often banned outright. Contacts don't solve it either; a single impact can shift or dislodge them

## Why Flow3 is the Athlete's Choice

Not all laser procedures are equal. Flow3 stands apart for three reasons that matter most to active people and competitive athletes:

**No corneal flap.** Standard LASIK procedures create a flap on the cornea that's folded back and repositioned. In contact sports—where impacts, collisions, and falls happen—that flap adds an extra layer of risk. Flow3 is a **surface-based laser procedure**. No flap is created at all. That's why militaries across the world prefer it.

**The cornea stays structurally stronger.** Because Flow3 doesn't create a flap, the cornea's structural integrity is preserved better. For an athlete, this means lower long-term risk, even after years of intense training and competition.

**One-week recovery for sports.** While your vision stabilises fully over 3 months, you can return to training and competition within days. Ekke was back on the mat in seven. Most athletes clear it within the first week—meaning you're not sidelined by the cure.
