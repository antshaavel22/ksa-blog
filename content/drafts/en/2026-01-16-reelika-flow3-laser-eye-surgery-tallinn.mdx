## The Drive Home That Changed Everything

On a summer afternoon, Reelika Tammeoriu walked out of KSA Silmakeskus in Tallinn without her glasses for the first time in years. The reason? Surprisingly straightforward: she wanted to drive comfortably. That single motivation unraveled a much bigger life change—one she's been quietly enjoying ever since.

Reelika spent nearly 13 years in England. What started as a brief stint became a life: London, a marketing degree, seven years in her chosen career. But when she and her partner moved back to Estonia, reality hit. A new home meant a driving test. A driving test meant a car. And a car, she realised, was impossible to navigate through Estonia's misty, rainy weather with fogged-up glasses.

## Why Glasses Don't Work in the Real World

Reelika had never loved wearing spectacles. On holiday, at work, in everyday moments—she'd squint her way through life, hoping no one noticed. But living in Estonia brought it into sharp focus. During driving lessons, masks made glasses steam up. Rain and snow turned the lenses into blurry obstacles. And more than anything, she wanted to see Estonia's landscape properly. "The nature here is simply beautiful," she says. "And I didn't want to experience it through a haze."

So she booked the pre-surgery assessment at KSA. Then she postponed it. Twice. Three times, in fact—until the driving test deadline loomed just a month away. That's when she decided: time to actually do this.

## The Procedure Itself

Reelika chose [Flow3 laser eye surgery](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/), the flapless surface laser technique that KSA specializes in. On the day, nerves crept in while selecting protective sunglasses in the waiting room. Then Dr. Ants Haavel appeared.

"He was brilliant," she recalls. "He distracted me, made me feel at ease. I even learned I have beautiful eye pigment!" What she'd imagined as a frightening ordeal turned out to be straightforward. The recovery period—wearing protective lenses—passed without drama. Professional, experienced, reassuring: that's how she describes the entire team.

## One Month Later: Driving Test Passed

Just 30 days after her [Flow3 procedure](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/), Reelika sat her driving exam. She passed. Her vision? "Five stars plus," she laughs.

Her partner loves looking into her eyes again. She loves seeing Estonian forests, lakes, and coastlines in crystal clarity. Driving has become genuinely enjoyable—no more frustration, no more compromise. And there's a hidden bonus: no more buying new glasses every two years.

That summer decision—born from the simple frustration of fogged lenses and a desire to see clearly—became the best investment in her quality of life.
