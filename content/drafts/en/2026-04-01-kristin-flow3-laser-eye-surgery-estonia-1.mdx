## A long time coming

Kristin Kerro describes herself as an active homemaker, health and wellness advocate, and content creator. But what really sets her story apart is this: it took her three attempts before she finally had vision correction surgery. Today, she's a certified personal trainer, physically active, and—most importantly—she wakes up and sees her children's faces clearly.

No glasses required.

## Glasses from childhood, limitations for life

Kristin was fitted for glasses in second grade. What began as mild discomfort became routine. Over the years, she tried contact lenses too, but her eyes reacted badly—irritation, dryness, the whole frustrating cycle.

Daily life with glasses felt restrictive. Workouts were awkward. But the moment that crystallised everything: giving birth. At the maternity ward, she didn't dare remove her glasses—her prescription was too strong. She needed them to see her newborn's face.

That realisation stuck with her. She wanted her vision back. Not corrected through lenses, but truly hers.

## Three times is the charm

Kristin's first attempts in Latvia went nowhere. The clinics told her laser vision correction wasn't an option for her.

Back in Estonia, she found [KSA Silmakeskus](https://ksa.ee/en/) in Tallinn. An initial eye examination revealed no serious contraindications—except for inflammation in one eye. She started treatment at Tartu University Hospital to clear it, then returned to KSA for reassessment.

Third time: everything checked out. The doctors gave her the green light.

## Recovery was faster than expected

Friends had warned her about a long, difficult recovery. It didn't happen.

Within days, Kristin was back to normal life. The initial haziness cleared quickly. Proper eye hydration helped. She kept her sunglasses on as advised—that mattered more than she'd expected.

The [Flow3 procedure](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) meant no flap, no cut. Just precise surface correction. Her body recovered accordingly.

## A completely different life

Training without glasses. Running. Gym workouts. The simple freedom of clear vision—that was the real gift.

Today, working as a certified personal trainer, she helps others build healthier lives. But first, she lived it herself. She can now see beyond her own immediate surroundings. The world isn't a blur beyond arm's length anymore.

Family time feels different. Playing outdoors feels different. Even health and wellness—her core values—feel more tangible when you can actually see the people you're training, the space you're moving through, the life you're building.

Her greatest dream was simple: wake up, see her children's faces, start the day.

No glasses on the nightstand. No frantic searching if someone moved them. Just clear vision, from the moment her eyes open.

That's her life now.
