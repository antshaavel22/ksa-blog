## Your vision is precious—and under attack every single day.

Not from anything dramatic. From the small, invisible choices we make without thinking. Too many hours staring at screens. Skipping sunglasses. Rubbing tired eyes. Missing regular check-ups. These habits feel harmless in the moment. But over months and years, they compound.

The hopeful part? Even small changes work. Your eyes are resilient. Once you know what's causing the damage, you can stop.

Here are seven habits—and how to break them.

## 1. Endless Screen Time

We live on our devices now. Computers at work, phones between meetings, tablets before bed. Hours of close-up focus does real damage: dryness, blur, headaches, neck strain—what doctors call "digital eye strain."

Your eyes aren't designed for this. They evolved to scan horizons, not pixels.

**The fix:** the 20-20-20 rule. Every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds. Simple. Free. Effective.

## 2. Going Without Sunglasses

UV radiation doesn't take weekends off. It penetrates cloud cover. It reflects off water and snow. Year-round exposure increases your risk of cataracts and macular degeneration—conditions that steal vision quietly, then all at once.

**The fix:** buy sunglasses that block at least 95% of UVA and UVB rays. Wear them every time you step outside. Not optional.

## 3. Rubbing Your Eyes

It's almost a reflex. Eyes feel tired, you rub them. Feels good for a second.

But repeated rubbing can damage your cornea and lead to keratoconus—a condition where the cornea thins and warps, distorting your vision permanently.

**Better option:** use a cool compress or artificial tears. Soothe without harm.

## 4. Sleeping in Contact Lenses

Unless they're specifically approved for overnight wear, contact lenses shouldn't touch your eyes while you sleep.

Why? Your cornea needs oxygen. When lenses block airflow for hours, infections and corneal injury become real risks.

**The rule:** remove them every night. Even if you're exhausted. It takes 10 seconds.

## 5. A Diet That Starves Your Eyes

Vision quality depends on what's on your plate. Vitamins A, C, and E. Lutein. Zinc. Omega-3s. Skip these, and your eyes decline faster.

**Foods that protect:** leafy greens, carrots, citrus, berries, flax seeds, algae oils. Nothing exotic. Real food, proven to work.

## 6. Skipping Eye Exams

Here's the danger: glaucoma and retinal disease don't announce themselves. They develop in silence. By the time you notice something wrong, damage is done.

**The standard:** get checked every two years. More often if you work at a screen or have family history of eye disease. These exams catch problems early, when they're still fixable.

## 7. Smoking (And Passive Smoke)

Smoking accelerates cataracts, macular degeneration, and optic nerve damage. Even secondhand smoke dries your eyes and compounds damage.

**The reality:** quitting works fast. Most people feel the benefit within weeks. Your vision will improve. So will everything else.
