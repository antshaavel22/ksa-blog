It seems counterintuitive that putting drops in your eye could dry it out. With most artificial tears, it would not. With medicated eye drops for high pressure and glaucoma, it often does. Up to 47% of patients on glaucoma drops report a burning sensation and dry eye. Knowing why turns the whole thing into a fixable problem.

## Two things working against your tear film

**The preservative.** To keep bacteria from growing in an opened bottle, most eye drops contain a chemical preservative. The most common one in glaucoma drops is benzalkonium chloride (BAK). BAK is good at killing bacteria in the bottle. It is also toxic to the cells on your eye surface. Daily exposure causes chronic inflammation and slowly destroys the tear film.

**The active ingredient.** On top of the preservative, the pressure-lowering medicine itself can change tear production and make dry eye worse.

A frustrating side effect is that patients buy artificial tears for relief, but if those tears also contain BAK or other preservatives, the situation gets worse, not better.

## Why this matters before laser surgery

Refractive surgery depends on a smooth, stable eye surface and very precise pre-op measurements. Chronic surface inflammation from BAK-preserved drops makes those measurements less reliable, and recovery slower.

Advanced glaucoma is generally a contraindication for refractive surgery, but mild, well-controlled cases are often candidates if the eye surface is healthy. The surface is the part you can fix.

## What to do

**1. Ask for preservative-free.** Switching to preservative-free glaucoma drops is the single most effective change. Studies show dry eye complaints drop from 47% to about 16% after the switch. This is a conversation with your glaucoma specialist.

**2. Use preservative-free artificial tears.** If your medicated drop already irritates the surface, do not add more preservatives on top of it. Preservative-free is the only sensible choice.

**3. Do not stop your medication.** Uncontrolled eye pressure can cause permanent vision loss. Manage the symptoms; do not stop the prescription.

**4. Coordinate care.** When you come to [KSA](https://ksa.ee), tell us every drop you put in your eyes. We work with your glaucoma specialist so the pressure stays safe and the surface gets the attention it needs.

Glaucoma drops are non-negotiable. The dry eye that comes with them often is. Get the preservative out of the equation, and most of the irritation goes with it.
