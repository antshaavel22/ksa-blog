## Back to School and Office: How to Care for Your Eyes

September's here, and most of us have settled into the school or office routine. That means less time outside and more time indoors—hunched over screens, surrounded by artificial light. Add the stress of routine back, and your eyes are under real pressure.

It doesn't have to be this way. Our team of optometrists at KSA Silmakeskus has compiled five straightforward strategies to keep your eyes healthy through demanding periods.

## 1. The 20-20-20 Rule

Staring at a screen for hours causes eye fatigue and dryness. Here's the fix: every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet (about 6 metres) away for 20 seconds. That's it. Your eye muscles relax, tension drops, and you reset your focus.

## 2. Keep Your Eyes Moist

When you focus on a screen, you blink less. Remind yourself to blink—it naturally lubricates your eyes. If dryness persists, eye drops can help, but speak to a specialist first. They'll recommend the right solution for your eyes rather than a generic fix.

## 3. Master Your Lighting

Proper lighting is non-negotiable for eye health. Position your desk so light doesn't glare off your screen. If daytime viewing feels exhausting, enable your computer's "night light" or blue light filter mode—it warms the screen colours, making them easier on your eyes. If you spend hours daily at screens, learn more about [digital eye strain](https://ksa.ee/en/computer-vision-syndrome/) and how to manage it effectively.

## 4. Position Matters

Your monitor should sit at or slightly below eye level, roughly 50–70 cm away. Keep your neck, spine, and shoulders straight. Adjust your chair so your feet rest flat on the floor. Bad posture multiplies eye strain.

## 5. Eat for Your Eyes

Your diet directly affects eye health. Load up on vitamin A, omega-3 fatty acids, and antioxidants: carrots, salmon, kale, spinach. Drink plenty of water too—dehydration affects your entire body, including your eyes.

## 6. Get Regular Eye Checks

Visit your ophthalmologist at least once a year. Early detection prevents serious problems down the line. If you wear glasses or contact lenses, ensure your prescription is current and still suits your needs.

## 7. Look After Your Lenses

Whether glasses or contacts, proper care extends their life and protects your eyes. Clean glasses with a microfibre cloth before each use. For contacts, always follow your optometrist's cleaning and storage instructions—no shortcuts.
