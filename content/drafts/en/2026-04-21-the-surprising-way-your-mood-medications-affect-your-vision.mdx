Looking after your mental health is one of the most worthwhile things you can do. A lot of patients I see manage anxiety, sleep or low mood with medication and feel like themselves again because of it. What they often do not know is that the same medication can quietly change how their eyes feel.

If you have started something new and the eyes are gritty, dry or unusually sensitive to light, it is worth knowing where that comes from.

## Why mood medications reach the eyes

Your tear glands take their cues from nerve signals, the same kind of signals that medication is designed to adjust. Many antidepressants, particularly older tricyclics like amitriptyline and nortriptyline, have what doctors call an anticholinergic effect. That means they block nerve signals to glands across the body, including the lacrimal glands. The glands quiet down, and the eye surface dries out.

Modern SSRIs like sertraline (Zoloft) work differently and have less of this effect, but they still cause dry eye in some patients.

## Beyond dryness

These medications also affect the small muscles inside the eye:

**Focus.** Sertraline and tricyclics can temporarily reduce accommodation, your eye's ability to switch focus between distances. Patients sometimes feel like they suddenly need reading glasses for their phone.

**Light sensitivity.** Some of these drugs slightly dilate the pupil. Bright sunshine becomes harder to deal with.

## Why this matters for laser surgery

Refractive surgery depends on very precise measurements of the eye, taken off the tear film. If the tear film is unstable from medication-induced dryness, the readings are less accurate, and the treatment less precise. After surgery, the eye needs a moist surface to heal smoothly. Chronic dryness makes recovery slower than it should be.

## What to do

**1. Do not stop on your own.** Suddenly stopping antidepressants or anxiety medication can be harmful. The eye complaints are almost always solvable without that.

**2. Talk to your prescribing doctor.** A small dose adjustment or a switch to a similar medication that is gentler on the eyes is sometimes possible.

**3. Use preservative-free drops.** Standard preservatives in eye drops can make inflammation worse over time. Preservative-free is the right default.

**4. Tell us what you take.** When you come in for a [consultation](https://ksa.ee), list every medication, even the ones that seem unrelated. We will plan around it.

Mental health and clear vision are not in conflict. Once we know the picture, we can prepare your eye surface properly and the procedure goes the way it should.
