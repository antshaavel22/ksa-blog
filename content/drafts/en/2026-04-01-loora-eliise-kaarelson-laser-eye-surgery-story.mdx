## Loora-Eliise Kaarelson: Laser eye surgery was one of the best decisions of my life

**Loora-Eliise Kaarelson is a singer whose presence commands both concert halls and television screens. She's starred in the TV series "Reetur" and the film "Tõde ja õigus", captivated audiences on "Eesti otsib superstaari", and performs weekly across Estonia with her musical duo Loora & Kauro Duo. Yet beneath these achievements lay a persistent challenge — myopia that made her already demanding schedule exhaustingly complicated.**

## Wearing contact lenses became exhausting

For over half her life, Loora-Eliise has corrected her vision. At –5 dioptres, contact lenses became her solution — practical for daily life and performances alike. But convenience has a shelf life. Over time, wearing lenses grew increasingly uncomfortable, especially during intense performance periods when her eyes never got a break. The result was predictable: chronic dryness, fatigue, and a growing frustration for an artist who needed to be at her best on stage.

She recalls a telling moment on a bitter winter day. Between performances, she decided to give her eyes relief by switching to glasses. But stepping from freezing air into a warm room caused her glasses to fog instantly — and the frame shattered. For the next two weeks, trapped in contact lenses without relief, she had no choice.

## The decision that changed everything

Laser eye surgery had been on Loora-Eliise's mind for years, but her prescription kept shifting. This spring, her latest eye examination revealed the news she'd been waiting for: her vision had finally stabilised. The timing felt right. She wanted to enjoy summer with clear vision, to perform with confidence, and to answer a question she'd asked herself countless times: "Will I actually be able to swim this summer and see?"

She chose [Flow3 laser eye surgery](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) — the flapless surface laser procedure performed at KSA Silmakeskus in Tallinn.

## Recovery and a new quality of life

Loora-Eliise had prepared herself for two full weeks of recovery. What surprised her was how quickly her eyes healed. By day four, she felt remarkable — so good she had to actively restrain herself from overdoing it. By day eight, she was fully back to her normal routine. The vision she'd been promised arrived exactly as expected.

More than technique or results, what matters most to Loora-Eliise now is freedom. No lenses drying out her eyes during performances. No foggy glasses on cold days. No compromise between her active life and her visual comfort.

"Laser eye surgery was one of the best decisions of my life," she says simply.
