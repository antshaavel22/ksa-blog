When you have a serious bacterial infection, antibiotics are some of the most useful drugs in medicine. Most of them have nothing to do with your eyes. A few specific families do, and the effects are worth knowing about because two of them are urgent.

## Three groups that affect the eye

**Fluoroquinolones.** Ciprofloxacin (Cipro) and levofloxacin (Levaquin), commonly used for respiratory or urinary tract infections. Research shows that oral fluoroquinolones are associated with a 4.5-fold increase in the risk of acute retinal detachment. The thinking is that these drugs alter the vitreous (the clear jelly that fills the eye), which then pulls on the retina (the light-sensitive back of the eye). Detachment usually happens within the first few days of treatment.

**Tetracyclines.** Tetracycline, doxycycline and minocycline are often prescribed long-term for acne and rosacea. With chronic use, they can cause idiopathic intracranial hypertension, a buildup of pressure in the fluid around the brain. The pressure squeezes the optic nerve, causing headaches, fluctuating vision and double vision. Minocycline can also permanently change the colour of the sclera (the white of the eye), leaving a blue-grey band.

**TB medications.** Ethambutol and isoniazid, prescribed together for tuberculosis, can be toxic to the optic nerve. The effects: optic neuritis, narrowed visual field, changes in colour vision. Without monitoring, the damage can become permanent.

## Why this matters before laser surgery

Refractive surgery depends on a stable optic nerve, an attached retina, and a settled eye surface. If you are actively on these specific antibiotics, the picture is moving, which is the wrong moment for elective surgery. We wait until you have finished treatment and your eye has fully settled.

## Warning signs to act on

**On a fluoroquinolone:** sudden flashes of light, a shower of new floaters, or a dark curtain falling across your vision. Get to an emergency eye doctor the same day. These are the signs of a retinal detachment.

**On doxycycline or minocycline for acne:** severe headaches or double vision. Contact your doctor and get your intracranial pressure checked.

**On long-term ethambutol:** regular, detailed eye exams are mandatory, not optional. Catching optic nerve toxicity early is what protects your vision.

## A practical plan

Do not stop a prescribed antibiotic early. Bacterial infections that come back after a half-finished course are often worse than the original. Tell us every antibiotic you are on or have been on recently when you come in for a [consultation](https://ksa.ee), and we will plan accordingly.
