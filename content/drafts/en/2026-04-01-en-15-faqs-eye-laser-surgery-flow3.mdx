## 15 Most Frequently Asked Questions About Eye Laser Surgery

**Considering eye laser surgery but still have doubts? You're not alone! We've compiled the 15 most common questions our clients ask before making their decision. This guide will help you understand how the procedure works, what to expect, and why Flow3 is today's most advanced laser vision correction option. By the end, you'll have a clear picture (pun intended!) of whether Flow3 is right for you.**

## 1. What is Flow3 laser surgery?

Flow3 is a bladeless, touchless laser procedure that uses the world's most advanced SCHWIND Amaris 1050RS laser to reshape your cornea. It corrects nearsightedness (myopia) and allows you to say goodbye to glasses and contact lenses.

## 2. Who is suitable for Flow3?

You're a good candidate if you:

- Are between 18–45 years old
- Have had stable vision for the past year
- Have healthy eyes with no serious eye conditions
- Want to become free from glasses and contact lenses

A pre-procedure eye assessment and consultation at KSA Silmakeskus will confirm your eligibility.

## 3. Is the procedure painful?

No – Flow3 is completely painless. We use advanced numbing eye drops to ensure your comfort. Based on client feedback, the most unusual sensation is simply the cool numbing drops trickling down your cheek.

## 4. How long does the procedure take?

The laser itself works for just 20–30 seconds per eye. The entire procedure, including preparation, takes 10–15 minutes. You'll spend about an hour at our clinic while we make final checks, help you choose sunglasses, and offer you tea, coffee, or a smoothie.

## 5. Will my vision be perfect after surgery?

Immediately after the procedure, your vision will be slightly blurred – we don't recommend driving. Most clients achieve the same sharpness they had with glasses within a few days to a week. Your vision continues to improve gradually during recovery, reaching its peak clarity (often 20/20 or better) over the healing period.

## 6. How long is the recovery period?

Most clients notice significant improvement by day three. Full recovery typically takes about 7 days, during which you'll use prescribed eye drops. We recommend avoiding bright light, swimming, and intense exercise during this week.

## 7. Can I return to work the next day?

We suggest avoiding screens for the first 3–4 days, though many clients prefer taking the full week off. We recommend you rest, take it easy, and spend as much time outdoors as possible – fresh air supports faster healing.

## 8. When can I exercise again?

Gentle activities like walking and yoga are fine after a few days. Once your protective lenses are removed (around day 7), all lifestyle restrictions end. Most clients return to their regular training routine, including intensive workouts, swimming, and contact sports.

## 9. What are the risks?

Flow3 is one of the safest vision correction procedures available. Serious complications are rare. The most common temporary side effects are dry eyes and mild glare, both manageable with prescribed drops and sunglasses. During your consultation, we'll discuss individual risk factors based on your eye health.

## 10. Why is Flow3 better than traditional LASIK?

Unlike traditional LASIK, Flow3 creates no corneal flap – the laser works directly on the surface. This means:

- Faster healing (about 1 week vs. 3–6 weeks)
- Better for active people and sports enthusiasts
- Lower risk of flap-related complications
- More corneal tissue preserved

## 11. How much does Flow3 cost?

Pricing varies based on your prescription strength. We offer flexible payment plans to make vision correction accessible. Contact us for a personalized quote after your eye assessment.

## 12. Will my prescription come back (regression)?

Regression is rare with Flow3, especially for moderate prescriptions. In some cases, minor adjustments may be needed, but enhancements are usually minimal. Your ophthalmologist will discuss long-term expectations during your consultation.

## 13. Can I have both eyes done at the same time?

Yes. We typically perform both eyes on the same day, with the second eye treated immediately after the first. This means one recovery period instead of two, and you regain clear vision in both eyes simultaneously.

## 14. What happens if I move my eye during the laser?

Don't worry – the SCHWIND Amaris 1050RS has sophisticated eye-tracking technology that follows your eye's movement 4,000 times per second. If you move beyond the safe range, the laser automatically pauses until alignment is restored.
