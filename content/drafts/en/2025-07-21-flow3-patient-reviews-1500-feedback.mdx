We ran an anonymous survey among nearly 1,500 patients who've had laser eye surgery at KSA Silmakeskus. The results? Remarkably honest. Remarkably positive. And worth sharing.

**86% of respondents had the [Flow3 procedure](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) — our flapless surface laser technique.** The remaining 14% had LASIK years earlier. So what you're reading is almost entirely about Flow3: the method our own clinical team chose for their own eyes.

We originally gathered this data for ourselves. But the feedback was so revealing that we decided to publish it. If you're considering laser eye surgery in Tallinn, this is the real story.

## Life Before Laser Surgery

Almost everyone in the survey had the same complaints about glasses and contact lenses:

- **57%** reported eye fatigue
- **45%** dealt with dry eyes
- **22–23%** suffered headaches and red eyes
- Only **24%** said they had no complaints at all

But the irritations went deeper. Patients mentioned frames that slipped constantly, lenses that fogged up, pressure marks on the nose bridge that became painful. Some developed skin problems from prolonged pressure. For many, it wasn't just discomfort — it was a daily frustration.

## Why People Chose Laser Surgery

When asked what drove them to book a procedure, the answers were clear:

- **80%** wanted to see well — simply that
- **70%** were tired of the inconvenience of glasses and lenses
- **44%** wanted better vision for sports or work
- **43%** had specific hobbies or careers demanding sharp vision
- **41%** finally had the means to afford it
- **40%** had stable prescription for at least two years
