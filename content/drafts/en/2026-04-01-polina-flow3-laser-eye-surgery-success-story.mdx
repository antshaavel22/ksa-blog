## About Polina

Polina works as an optometry assistant at KSA Silmakeskus, helping conduct vision tests and preparing patients before their laser procedures. She loves the human side of the work—seeing people's joy when results exceed their expectations. And yes, she's also had the procedure herself.

Outside work, Polina swims, walks in nature, and works with ceramics. A big part of her life is dog training and competition preparation—a pursuit that demands dedication, patience, and precision.

![Polina at KSA](https://ksa.ee/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/img_7174.jpg)

## Years Behind Glasses

Polina got her first pair of glasses at 15. She rarely wore contact lenses—mostly just for sports or special occasions. What bothered her most about glasses? The tedium of the same frame day after day.

"When you wear the same glasses every single day, the frame drives you mad. You end up wanting new ones constantly—but that gets expensive fast," she says.

Contact lenses weren't the answer either. "They made my eyes dry, and every morning took forever just to get them in properly."

One story stands out from her glasses-wearing years: "A few years back, I went on holiday with a friend and her child. I carelessly left my glasses on a shelf, and the kid broke them in half. I had no backup pair and couldn't use contact lenses yet. For the rest of the five-day trip, I couldn't see people's faces properly. I had to relive the whole experience later through photos."

![Polina at work](https://ksa.ee/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/img_7157-scaled-1.jpg)

Since most of KSA's team has had laser correction, Polina didn't hesitate when the opportunity came her way. "When I realised I could do this, I didn't think twice. I booked my [Flow3 procedure](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) right away."

![Polina smiling](https://ksa.ee/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/img_2319-copy.jpg)

## Recovery: Smooth and Manageable

Recovery unfolded calmly. Before the procedure, her distance vision was poor and close-up sharp. In the first few days after, it flipped—distance was crystal clear, but near vision was blurry.

"That lasted about four days, then everything came into focus—both far and near," Polina explains. The days passed at an easy pace. "I walked a lot and handled household tasks. The main difference was I barely used my phone or computer. That digital detox actually felt really good."

![Polina recovering at home](https://ksa.ee/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/img_4205-copy.jpg)

## A Story Worth Sharing

The first glasses-free days were an adjustment. "Even after a few days without them, I'd still reach to adjust them out of habit, and take them off at night."

Now, her favourite freedom? Swimming pools. "No more glasses at the pool. I can dive and hit the sauna without worrying they'll get in the way or break."

For anyone hesitant or worried about the procedure, she's direct: "I know many people fear that laser eye surgery is risky. But working here at KSA, I've seen thousands of patients who chose this path. Our team—the people who know this best—we all chose [Flow3](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) for our own eyes. That says everything.

"The procedure takes minutes. Recovery is fast. And your life changes. It's worth it."
