**Actress, director and singer Adeele Jaago dreamed of ditching her glasses for years. Finally, she found the courage to book [Flow3 laser eye surgery](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) at KSA Silmakeskus. What she expected and what actually happened turned out to be two very different things. Her vision has improved beyond expectation — and so has her life.**

## Myopia: embarrassing moments and lost focus

Adeele has worn glasses since her teenage years. Though her prescription wasn't severe, short-sightedness created constant awkward situations.

"One summer, I arranged to meet a close friend on the street. I wasn't wearing my glasses, and when I spotted someone walking towards me from a distance, I got genuinely excited. I started waving and running towards her with open arms. A few metres away, I realised — this wasn't my friend at all. To save face, I just kept going with the enthusiasm and ran straight past this complete stranger. When she looked back, she saw me running towards absolutely nothing. I had to hide around the corner from sheer embarrassment."

On stage, the blurred world created real problems. After years working in theatre, Adeele experienced constant frustration from her myopia. "I've basically watched my scene partners through a fog. I've stumbled around in the dark backstage. Contact lenses would shift out of place — and that's just the beginning of the complications."

She remembers moments when a fellow actor would look at her from across the stage and she'd have to squint hard just to see them properly. "Being on stage without glasses or contacts felt like a major handicap. When everything's blurry, you can't concentrate. It's incredibly uncomfortable."

## Expectation versus reality on procedure day

Adeele chose KSA Silmakeskus because the clinic has decades of experience and genuine recommendations from people she trusted. "I have several close friends and colleagues who've had successful procedures here. Hearing about their experiences gave me the confidence to overcome my doubts. I decided to give myself the biggest gift I could — clear, sharp vision."

She prepared thoroughly for the day. One thing she got completely wrong: the time commitment. "I thought the procedure would take hours. I planned my trip to Tallinn with way too much buffer time. Within an hour of finishing, I was already free. I actually laughed at myself and ended up killing time at the clinic. The wonderful staff at KSA were patient with me and helped arrange transport whenever I was ready — they even have a taxi service built in. I sat there with sunglasses on, sipping a smoothie, listening to podcasts I'd queued up."

The whole day looked nothing like what Adeele had imagined. She'd expected to spend a long recovery period in some quiet room, being gently escorted to a waiting taxi. "In reality, I was back home in Viljandi by evening."

## The surprise of genuine results

What has surprised Adeele most is how naturally the whole thing happened. The recovery was faster than she anticipated. The vision itself — sharper than she'd ever experienced, even with glasses.

"I remember the first morning waking up and being able to see clearly without reaching for anything. That's something people who've never worn glasses might not appreciate. For me, it felt like magic."

On stage now, everything is different. She can see her scene partners clearly from any distance. She moves with confidence in the dark. No more squinting, no more fog, no more contact lens emergencies.

"It genuinely changed how I work and how I feel on stage. I'm more present. Less anxious about what I'm missing. It sounds like a small thing, but when you've worked around a visual handicap for decades, suddenly being free of it is genuinely life-changing."
