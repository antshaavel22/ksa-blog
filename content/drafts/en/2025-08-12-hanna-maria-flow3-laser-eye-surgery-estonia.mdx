## A Life Through Glass

Hanna-Maria has been wearing glasses since childhood. For her, they weren't just a vision aid—they were part of her identity. "I actually liked wearing glasses," she recalls. "They felt like part of who I was, sometimes even replacing makeup."

But glasses came with their own frustrations. Summers in Estonia meant constant switching between prescription and sunglasses. "Sun comes out, then clouds roll in—glasses case out, regular frames in, sunglasses back on," she laughs. "It gets old fast."

Contact lenses offered temporary relief, but eventually became uncomfortable and left her eyes dry. The real problem emerged when she picked up tennis seriously. Glasses slipped down her nose mid-game. Her friends had started copying her habit of pushing them back up. After years of this, the novelty wore off.

## The Turning Point: Motherhood

Everything changed when her son was born. Suddenly, glasses weren't just inconvenient—they were impractical.

"Once he arrived, I realised I needed better vision," Hanna-Maria explains. "He'd constantly grab at my frames, pull them off, get fingerprints all over the lenses. They'd break regularly. It became exhausting."

She'd been thinking about vision correction for years, but motherhood made the decision clear. Life with a young child needed freedom from these daily hassles.

## Recovery: Fast and Straightforward

The first week after her [Flow3 procedure](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) was the crucial phase. Unlike traditional LASIK—which involves creating a flap—Flow3 works on the eye's surface, meaning faster healing and less risk during sports or accidental contact.

Hanna-Maria followed the post-care instructions carefully. Within days, the world came into sharp focus. By the end of the first week, her vision had stabilised significantly. Within a month, she was back to normal activities, including tennis.

Motherhood has become easier too. Her son can't knock off glasses that don't exist. Summer doesn't mean switching between two pairs anymore.

But perhaps the most telling detail: Hanna-Maria's entire life has kept pace with her clearer vision. She's more active, less frustrated, and honestly—more herself.
