## Why December is the Busiest Month at Our Clinic

December is always busy at KSA Silmakeskus. Record numbers of patients walk through our doors in the final weeks of the year—and there's a clear reason why.

The Christmas period combines practical advantage with something deeper: the chance to step into a new year with new vision. For many who've worn glasses or contact lenses for years, this is the moment they decide enough is enough.

## The Practical Advantage: Time Off That Actually Works

According to optometrist Liisi Mölder, the most straightforward reason is timing. "The extended holiday period means you don't need to take separate leave or sick days for recovery," she explains. "You can blend the healing process naturally into your time off. And for those who work from home, you can pace your recovery around your own schedule."

This is a real advantage. The [Flow3 procedure](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) requires only about a week for meaningful recovery—and if that week falls during your Christmas break, you're covered.

## The Deeper Reason: A New Year, A New You

But there's more to it than logistics.

For many people, glasses and contact lenses feel like an ongoing obligation. At some point, they decide: that's it. This is my gift to myself—clear vision, and everything that comes with it: convenience, comfort, freedom.

Liisi points to the psychological pull of the new year: "Many people treat this like they treat other resolutions. They want to leave glasses behind in the old year and enter the new one with clear sight. If others are planning to hit the gym or eat better, glasses wearers make a vision gift to themselves—and they time the surgery for the holidays so their eyes are fully healed by January."

## Winter Light: A Hidden Advantage

There's also a practical detail that works in your favor this time of year.

"Outside, it's dark enough," Liisi notes. "Recovery is actually easier when your eyes don't have to handle intense sunlight or bright daylight. That's a real advantage in December."

Winter also brings dry, itchy eyes—something contact lens wearers and glasses wearers both know well. "If you have surgery before the worst cold hits, your eyes will be fully healed before winter's harshest weeks. Then snow, frost, and winter scenery are just beautiful to experience—with no concerns about fogged-up lenses or dry eyes."
