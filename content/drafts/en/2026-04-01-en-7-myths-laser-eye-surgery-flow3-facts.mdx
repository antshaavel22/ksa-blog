Over the years, laser vision correction has gained remarkable popularity, yet myths surrounding it can create confusion. Today, we're debunking seven of the most common misconceptions.

## Myth 1: Laser Procedure Results Aren't Permanent

One widespread myth is that the benefits of laser eye surgery don't last forever. In reality, laser correction permanently reshapes your cornea, resulting in improved vision. This change is permanent and cannot be undone. In rare cases, some refractive error may return, but this is typically caused by changes in the eye's lens over time – not failure of the procedure. However, like every other part of your body, your cornea ages naturally. This is something no laser procedure can prevent. It means that as you age, your vision may inevitably change. But this doesn't mean the procedure's effect disappears; it simply means your eyes are aging naturally, as they would have without intervention. **[Want to know more about vision years after laser surgery? ## Myth 2: You'll Smell Burning During the Procedure

During the procedure, you may notice a distinctive odour, but there's no need to worry – it doesn't indicate burning or scorching. The lasers used are designed so they create virtually no heat in the tissue, eliminating any possibility of burning. In fact, this peculiar smell is caused by a chemical reaction that occurs when the laser meets your cornea, releasing carbon atoms into the air. It's a completely natural process and nothing to be concerned about.

## Myth 3: Laser Surgery Can Cause Blindness

One common fear about eye surgery is that it might cause blindness. Since laser correction focuses only on reshaping the eye's surface, the risk of serious complications is extremely low. That said, like any medical procedure, laser eye surgery does carry certain risks – but these are rare. The primary concern is inflammation, and KSA Silmakeskus places great emphasis on prevention. ## Myth 7: Younger Eyes Don't Need Correction Surgery

Some people assume laser surgery is only for older individuals struggling with presbyopia. In reality, laser correction benefits anyone with refractive errors like myopia, hyperopia, or astigmatism – regardless of age.
