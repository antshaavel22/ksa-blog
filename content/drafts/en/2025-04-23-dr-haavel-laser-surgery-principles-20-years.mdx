## One Principle That Hasn't Changed in Twenty Years

When Dr. Ants Haavel founded KSA Silmakeskus in 2005, the clinic's philosophy rested on a single, unwavering principle: never do more to the eye than is genuinely necessary.

This didn't come from textbooks or conference presentations. It came from clinical experience — from watching eyes that had been overtreated, operated on too aggressively. Eyes that were approached as technical problems rather than as one of the body's most precious sensory organs.

"I saw patients return with corneal ectasia," Dr. Haavel recalls. "It's a condition you can't fix with glasses or contact lenses. People came hoping to escape spectacles, and left with a far more serious problem. That was never an outcome I could accept."

## Why Choose a Flap-Free Approach

When Dr. Haavel entered refractive surgery, he faced a choice that flew against the grain of the time. LASIK clinics marketed rapid recovery — patients often saw well the next day. Surface ablation meant slower recovery, more patient oversight, and genuine patience.

But the evidence was clear. Studies show LASIK leaves the cornea with only 2–28% of its original strength. The risk of ectasia jumps roughly 4.5 times higher with LASIK than with surface ablation. A [flap-free method like Flow3](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) avoids creating an irreversible cut in the cornea — so the cornea's natural structural integrity stays intact.

## The Number That Defines Our Work: Zero

55,000 eyes. Over 20 years. Zero ectasia cases. Zero surgically induced keratoconus.

According to Dr. Haavel, this isn't luck: "A track record of 55,000 operated eyes without a single such complication is one of the most reliable safety indicators in refractive surgery across the Baltics. It comes from three things working in concert — rigorous pre-operative screening, careful patient selection, and a flap-free surgical method."

At KSA Silmakeskus, the pre-operative assessment takes a full hour and includes thousands of measurements — corneal topography, thickness mapping, and biomechanical analysis. Around 15% of people who walk in hoping for surgery hear a 'no' — because the cornea is too thin, the topography is unstable, or expectations don't match reality. That honesty matters just as much as surgical precision.

## What Twenty Years Have Taught Us

Patience is part of the equation. Recovery after a flap-free procedure takes time. The first week can feel blurry, and full visual stability typically emerges over two to three months — sometimes a bit longer. But that's the price worth paying to preserve corneal structure and avoid long-term complications.

"Every patient who chooses [Flow3](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) knows the recovery timeline upfront," Dr. Haavel explains. "And they accept it, because they understand what they're protecting: their eye's natural strength and their vision for life."

The clinical team at KSA chose Flow3 for their own eyes. That decision says everything.

If you're considering laser vision correction, understand that the safest outcome isn't always the fastest one. It's the one built on experience, honesty, and a commitment to doing only what your eye truly needs.
