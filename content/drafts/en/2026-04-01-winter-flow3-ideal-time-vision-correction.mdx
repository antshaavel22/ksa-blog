## The Perfect Window

Christmas, New Year, and early January—these weeks tend to be quieter at work. Most people can arrange a few extra days off. That's precisely why many choose this season to finally address their vision.

The [Flow3 procedure](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) requires roughly seven days of recovery. While it might sound like a long stretch, our patients consistently tell us they're grateful for it. Those days give them proper rest, sleep, and time to let their eyes settle in peace.

## Why Winter Wins at KSA

For years now, December and January have been our busiest months. Students are on holiday from university. Estonians living abroad return home to visit family. Working professionals can take time off. Add the public holidays into the mix, and the calendar suddenly cooperates.

But there's something deeper here too. Many people who spend their summers pursuing sports, hiking, swimming, or outdoor hobbies don't want glasses or contact lenses holding them back. They'd rather tackle vision correction when the world slows down—and winter is when that happens.

## A New Year, New Vision

Letting go of glasses and contact lenses is, for many, a genuine dream. But dreams need action. We've noticed that December and January attract far more diagnostic visits and [Flow3 procedures](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) than any other months. These are the weeks when people think about fresh starts, about what they truly want, about the year ahead.

There's something powerful about beginning a new year having already fulfilled one of your dreams.
