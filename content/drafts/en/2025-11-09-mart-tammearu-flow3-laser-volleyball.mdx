Meet Märt Tammearu—24, professional volleyball player, and a mainstay of the Estonian national team. Originally from Rabavere, he's played for clubs across Belgium, France, and Poland. For the past three years, he's been named Estonia's best male volleyball player.

When competition season arrives, life revolves around training and matches. But during summer breaks in Estonia, you'll find him on beach volleyball courts and disc golf courses. Sport isn't just what he does—it's who he is. Recently, Märt made a decision that would reshape his daily life: he underwent the [Flow3 laser procedure](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) at KSA Silmakeskus.

## Glasses, Contact Lenses, and Sport Don't Mix

Markt got his first pair of glasses six years ago when his vision started holding him back—both in the classroom and on court. Glasses and contact lenses quickly became daily headaches. Not just in professional volleyball, but during military service too, where wearing lenses in the forest was a no-go.

"At the shooting range, you really want to see where your target is," he explains, naming one of the key reasons he started looking for a solution.

One training camp incident stands out:

"After shooting practice, we broke for lunch. I took my glasses off and set them down next to my bag to rest. Next thing I know, I've sat on them—snapped instantly. At least it was one of the last days of service," Märt recalls with a laugh.

Volleyball brought its own disasters. "During a warm-up for a national team friendly, I got hit in the face with a ball and my lens flew out. The warm-up ended with me sprinting back to the hotel room to grab a replacement, then sprinting back to the game," he grins.

## Three Months: The Window He Needed

This year, Märt's volleyball season wrapped early—leaving him with a rare gift: a three-month break before national team training resumed mid-summer. Perfect timing for vision correction.

He'd heard about KSA Vision Clinic through his teammate Robert Täht, who'd already had the procedure done and couldn't stop recommending it. Märt didn't overthink it:

"I booked an eye exam, got the green light, and the decision was made."

## Recovery Came Faster Than Expected

The recovery was gentler than Märt had imagined. The first four days were the toughest—blurry vision, watery eyes, that gritty sensation. What got him through: his partner's support, podcasts on loop, and time outdoors.

By day seven, when the protective lenses came off, he was back to normal life—most importantly, back to training. One month later, his vision was completely stable.

## Life After Flow3

"Now I live without thinking about my eyes," Märt says. "Before, glasses or lenses were always there, always a problem. Now? I just play."
