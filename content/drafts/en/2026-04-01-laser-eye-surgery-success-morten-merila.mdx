## Who Benefits Most From Laser Eye Surgery?

The answer: active, young people who live on the move. People who run, swim, hike—who refuse to let glasses or contacts slow them down. People who chase experiences, seek adventure, and want to truly see the world around them.

That's Morten Merila.

Why did Morten need multiple pairs of glasses? What drove him to organize some of Estonia's biggest investment conferences? And can eye surgery really be considered an investment? Read on.

## A Life in Motion

"I spend as much time outdoors as I can," Morten explains. "Running, swimming, hiking—those are my constants. I have homes in Saaremaa and Võrumaa, and I'm always drawn back to Tartu. I'm always hunting for small adventures, experiences, events that make everyday life richer. Weekend nights in the forest. Concerts in unexpected places. Sailing on the sea."

Before his recent sabbatical, Morten spent nearly four years at Äripäev (Estonia's leading business publication) managing their investment conferences and seminars. His days were consumed by organizing conferences, seminars, training sessions—and running the Investment Club. "I needed to step back, rest, and gather my thoughts before moving toward my next challenge," he says.

## Learning From the Best

Before joining Äripäev, Morten had heard about investing—friends did it, colleagues mentioned it—but it felt distant. Everything changed when he joined the stock market editorial team.

"Suddenly, I was immersed in financial news every single day. Within weeks, I made my first investments. My colleagues were investors themselves. They wrote about markets. They lived it," Morten recalls. "And through organizing events, I got to hear from well-known investors and entrepreneurs firsthand. That kind of mentorship is invaluable."

But his timing tested him immediately. Morten started just as COVID-19 hit in 2020.

"Conferences and seminars had never been fully digital before. Nobody had done it. Suddenly, we had to adapt in real time, within days," he remembers. "One major event I led was the Investor Toomas conference. It had always been in-person only. In early 2021, restrictions forced us to move the entire thing online in just two weeks. We realized the hybrid model was here to stay."

That experience—constant change, moving fast, solving problems in real time—shaped how he thinks about investment in general.

## Why Glasses Were Never an Option for His Lifestyle

Morten wore glasses for years. Multiple pairs. One for the office, perhaps another for sports. The friction was constant.

"When you're moving between Saaremaa, Võrumaal, Tartu, organizing events, running, swimming—glasses become an obstacle," he explains. "You worry about them falling off. You can't see properly in sunlight. In water, they're useless. It's a small thing until it becomes a daily annoyance."

He'd heard about [laser eye surgery](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) from colleagues and friends, but the decision took time. Then he learned about [Flow3](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/)—the flapless surface laser procedure.

"What impressed me was the safety profile," Morten says. "No flap. No incision. It's designed for active people—anyone who plays sports, anyone who can't afford downtime. The recovery is quick. Within a week, you're back to normal."

He also learned that the clinical team at KSA Silmakeskus had chosen Flow3 for their own eyes. "That told me everything I needed to know," he says. "If the surgeons trust it enough for their own vision, why wouldn't I?"
