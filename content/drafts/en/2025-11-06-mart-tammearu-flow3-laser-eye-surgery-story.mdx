## Glasses, contacts and sport — a difficult mix

Märt got his first pair of glasses six years ago, when his vision started affecting him both at school and on the court. For a professional athlete, glasses and contact lenses became a constant source of frustration — and even during his military service, contact lenses were off-limits in the field. "When you're shooting, you want to actually *see* where the target is," Märt points out.

One training session in the woods left him particularly exasperated. "After our shooting drills, we had a break. I took off my glasses and set them down next to my kit to rest. Next thing I knew, I'd sat on them. Completely smashed," he recalls with a wry smile. "At least it was one of the last days of service."

Incidents on the volleyball court were equally frustrating. "During a warm-up at an international friendly, I got hit in the face with the ball and my contact lens flew out. The warm-up ended with a sprint back to the hotel room — pop in a new lens, back to the game," he laughs.

## Three months off — the perfect timing

When Märt's club season ended early this year, and national team training wasn't starting until mid-summer, he suddenly had a rare gift: three consecutive months free. The perfect window to address his vision problem once and for all.

He found KSA Silmakeskus through a teammate, Robert Tähe, who'd had the procedure done and spoke highly of it. Märt didn't overthink it. "I just booked the consultation. When they gave me the green light, that was it — I was in."

## Recovery: faster than expected

According to Märt, the recovery was much smoother than he'd anticipated. The first four days were the toughest — his vision was blurry, his eyes watered, and there was a gritty feeling. His partner kept him company, he listened to podcasts, and spent time in fresh air. By day seven, when the protective contact lenses came off, he was back to normal life. Most importantly: back to training. Within a month, his vision had fully stabilized.

## Life after the procedure

"Now I walk around exactly the same way, but I can actually *see* everything — and it still gives me that 'wow' feeling," Märt says of his new life.

The improvements are concrete. On the court, he reads the game faster. Off it, he doesn't waste mental energy managing glasses or contacts. In the field, during drills, on the beach playing volleyball — everywhere, his vision is simply there. No compromises.
