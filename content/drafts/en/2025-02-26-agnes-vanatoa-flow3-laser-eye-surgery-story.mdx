## Meet Agnes: Sales Lead and Now, Glasses-Free

Agnes runs the sales department at KSA Silmakeskus. She manages people, drives results, and genuinely invests in her team's growth. Outside work, she's constantly moving—HIIT training, outdoor activities, travel. For 15 years, though, there was one constant friction in her life: glasses and contact lenses.

Then she decided to change that.

## Glasses Since Age 12

Agnes got her first pair of glasses at 12. By her early twenties, she'd switched to contact lenses. Fifteen years later, they were second nature—but never convenient.

"The worst moments were when I'd lose one lens," she recalls. "Then you're carrying around backup sets, solution bottles, cases. You're always thinking about it."

One story stands out. During a long trip through Mexico, her lenses ran out—and the replacements she found locally were far too weak. "Half the trip, I was basically blind," she laughs.

## Why Now?

Working at an eye clinic had its advantage: Agnes knew the solutions. But what finally tipped the scales was something simpler.

"I wanted to enjoy sport and travel without constant planning. I wanted to live without worrying whether I had everything I needed to see properly. Freedom, basically."

## One Week to 100%

Agnes had the [Flow3 procedure](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/)—our flapless surface laser technique. What happened next surprised even her.

"Within one week, my vision was 100%. No pain, no real discomfort. The only thing I noticed for a couple of days was slight cloudiness at close range." She took it easy that first week (no intense workouts, no staring at screens for hours), but she walked, cooked, spent time with friends. Life continued.

## The Small Freedoms Add Up

It's the little things that matter most to her now.

"Going to bed without wondering if I've taken out my lenses. Being able to nap spontaneously in the afternoon—something that was almost impossible before. Those tiny moments of freedom matter more than you'd think."

Agnes is someone who values independence. Removing the dependence on corrective lenses—on remembering, carrying, managing—aligned perfectly with how she wants to live.

## Her Advice

For anyone hesitating, fear holding them back, Agnes has one message:

"Do it, even if you're scared."
