## Add 40 Kilometres to Your Vision

Glaucoma damages the optic nerve — the connection between your eye and brain. After cataracts, it's the second leading cause of blindness worldwide.

Here's the puzzle: we still don't know what causes it. Environment? Diet? Probably both.

But we do know how to protect yourself. Eat one serving of leafy greens — kale, spinach, lettuce — at least once a week. This cuts your glaucoma risk by 69%. Carrots earn silver medal status (eat them weekly). Peaches take bronze.

## The Pigments That Guard Your Eyes

Lutein and zeaxanthin are yellow compounds found mainly in dark leafy vegetables. They're not just passive shields — they actively improve vision clarity.

Lycopene, the red pigment in tomatoes, travels straight to your prostate after you eat it, protecting against cancer there. Beta-carotene in sweet potatoes and carrots accumulates in your ovaries, guarding against ovarian cancer.

But lutein and zeaxanthin? They go exactly where they're needed: your retina.

## The 40-Kilometre Difference

Here's what makes this remarkable.

Lutein and zeaxanthin absorb light most efficiently from blue wavelengths — the part of the spectrum that scatters easiest in haze and distance. Stand on a hillside on a hazy day. Someone eating lutein and zeaxanthin-rich foods regularly? They can see up to 40 kilometres further than someone whose retina contains low levels of these pigments.

That's not metaphorical. That's measurable.

## What This Means for Your Eyes

Vision health starts before any procedure. Nutrition matters. So does regular screening, especially if glaucoma runs in your family.

If you've already developed refractive error — myopia, hyperopia, astigmatism — dietary support won't reverse it. That's where [laser eye surgery](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) or [lens replacement options](https://ksa.ee/en/icb-time) come in.

But if you want to protect what you have? Eat your greens. Your retina will thank you.
