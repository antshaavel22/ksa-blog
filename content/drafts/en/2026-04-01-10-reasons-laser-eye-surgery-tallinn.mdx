## 10 Reasons to Choose Laser Eye Surgery

Wondering if vision correction is worth it? Whether you've worn glasses your whole life or switched to contacts years ago, there comes a point when many people ask: is there a better way?

At KSA Silmakeskus—Estonia's leading laser eye surgery clinic—we've performed over 55,000 procedures. We see the real reasons people choose to ditch their frames and lenses. Here are 10 that keep coming up.

### 1. You play sports seriously (or just want to)

Contact sports and glasses don't mix. Boxing, rugby, martial arts—one wrong move and your frames snap or fall off. Even non-contact sports cause friction: fogged-up ski goggles, a lost contact lens mid-volleyball match, chlorine stinging your eyes at the pool.

After [laser eye surgery](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/), these worries vanish. Full vision. No obstacles. Just you and your sport.

### 2. Your eyes have stopped tolerating contacts

You managed fine for years. Then suddenly—dryness, redness, discomfort. Your eyes are telling you something.

Contacts work brilliantly for about 15 years. After that, many people find they just can't wear them anymore. Pushing through the irritation risks infections and other complications that are easily avoided. Vision correction offers a permanent out.

### 3. Your vision isn't sharp where it matters

Night driving troubles you. Highway visibility feels fuzzy. Or your distance vision simply isn't as crisp as you'd like—even with your current prescription.

Laser correction often solves exactly these issues. Worth a consultation with a specialist to explore your options.

### 4. Headaches are a regular visitor

Many glasses and contact lens wearers experience headaches from eye strain. Sometimes the prescription isn't quite right. Sometimes frames press on your nose and temples. Sometimes it's the constant tiny adjustments your eyes make to compensate.

Remove the cause. Remove the headaches.

### 5. You want your time back

Think about the cumulative hours: buying new glasses, replacing lost contacts, cleaning solutions, storage cases, insurance claims, regular eye tests just to update your prescription.

Laser vision correction eliminates all that—for years, possibly for life. That's freedom most people don't realize they're missing until they have it.

### 6. You prefer how you look without glasses

Some people love wearing frames—they're stylish, iconic, part of who they are. But many others have spent years wishing they could see their own face clearly in the mirror. Wishing their eyes were visible in photos. Wishing they felt more like themselves.

After vision correction, patients often describe it as getting a new version of their own face back. That confidence boost is real.

### 7. Your prescription has stabilized

Laser correction works best when your vision isn't still changing. If you're under 18 or your prescription shifts significantly year to year, you'll typically want to wait. But once it stabilizes—usually by your mid-20s—you're a candidate.

Ask your eye care specialist: is my vision stable enough?

### 8. You're tired of depending on corrections

Some people describe it differently: independence. Not needing anything between your eye and the world. No monthly costs. No forgotten glasses at home. No backup pairs. Just waking up and seeing clearly.

It's a form of freedom that's hard to explain until you experience it.

### 9. You've researched and found a clinic you trust

Not all laser eye surgery is the same. At KSA, our entire clinical team—including our surgeons—chose [Flow3 laser correction](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) for their own eyes. That's not marketing. That's the strongest possible vote of confidence.

We've also performed over 55,000 procedures under the leadership of Dr. Ants Haavel, an ophthalmologist with 20+ years of experience. When your surgical team is willing to have the same procedure done on their own eyes, that tells you something.

### 10. You want a solution that lasts

Contacts need replacing. Glasses frames break and prescriptions change. But vision correction—whether through [Flow3](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) (our flapless surface laser technique) or [ICB lens replacement](https://ksa.ee/en/icb-time) (for those not suitable for laser)—delivers long-term results.

Not temporary fixes. Lasting vision.
