## Understanding Why Eye Drops Fail

Why does this happen? The simple answer is that not all dry eye is the same. If you do not know which type of dry eye you have, you might be treating the wrong problem.

Let's break down the mechanics of your tears, why standard treatments like punctum plugs are not always the magic fix, and how KSA Vision Clinic's holistic approach can help you finally find lasting relief.

## Aqueous vs. Evaporative: What Is Really Happening in Your Eye?

To understand why your eyes are dry, we need to look at your tear film. Your tears are incredibly complex and are made up of different layers. The two most important layers for keeping your eyes comfortable are the water (aqueous) layer and the oil (lipid) layer.

Because of these two distinct layers, dry eye is generally classified into two main types:

**Aqueous-Deficient Dry Eye.** This happens when your tear glands simply do not produce enough water. Your eyes are genuinely "dry" because there is a lack of liquid volume.

**Evaporative Dry Eye.** This is the most common form of the condition. In this case, your eyes might be producing plenty of water, but the meibomian glands in your eyelids are failing to produce enough protective oil. Without this oil layer sitting on top of the water to seal it in, your tears evaporate into the air almost instantly.

## Why Punctum Plugs Aren't Always Enough

If you suffer from the first type—a lack of water—a tiny device called a punctum plug can be a miracle worker. By placing a small, painless plug into your tear drain, it acts just like a stopper in a bathtub, trapping whatever tears you do produce on the surface of your eye so they cannot drain away into your nose.

However, if your main problem is evaporative dry eye (a lack of oil), punctum plugs alone are rarely sufficient. Think about it: if your tears lack their protective oil layer, they will evaporate too quickly into the surrounding air regardless of whether the physical drain is plugged. Your eye will simply dry out from the surface up.

This is exactly why your eye drops disappear so fast—they are evaporating because your eye is missing its natural oil shield!
