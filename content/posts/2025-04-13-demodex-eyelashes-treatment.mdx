## You're Not Alone: Demodex Mites Are Everywhere

Your body hosts countless bacteria and microscopic organisms—most of them harmless, and all of them invisible to the naked eye. Among these invisible residents is a tiny creature called *Demodex*, which lives in the follicles of your eyelashes.

If you're healthy, you'll never notice it's there. You could go your whole life without knowing your eyelashes have these microscopic neighbours.

But sometimes, things change.

## When Demodex Becomes a Problem

Problems arise when Demodex mites multiply beyond normal levels. When that happens, you might experience:

- Itching and irritation on the eyelid
- Red, inflamed eyelid margins
- A sensation of something on your eyelashes
- Dry eye symptoms
- Blurred or hazy vision

It's uncomfortable. It's frustrating. But here's the good news: it's also manageable.

## The Treatment Is Simpler Than You'd Think

Demodex-related eyelid inflammation responds well to a straightforward three-part approach:

**1. Clean your bedding regularly.** Wash pillowcases, sheets, and any fabric touching your face in hot water. This removes mites and prevents reinfection.

**2. Reset your eye makeup routine.** Replace old makeup products and avoid wearing eye makeup until symptoms resolve. This sounds drastic, but it works—old products can harbor mites.

**3. Use prescribed medication and daily lid hygiene.** Your eye specialist will recommend a topical treatment for your eyelids, combined with gentle daily cleansing using tea tree oil. This combination tackles inflammation directly.

Most people see improvement within weeks. The mites and your eyes can coexist peacefully again.

## Don't Confuse It With Something Else

Demodex-related eyelid inflammation is common in both children and adults. The trouble is, it's often misdiagnosed. Many people receive treatment for dry eye syndrome or conjunctivitis when the real culprit is Demodex.

If your symptoms aren't improving with standard dry eye treatment, ask your eye specialist to check for Demodex. A simple, targeted approach can make all the difference.
