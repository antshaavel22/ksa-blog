## From Spectacles to Freedom: One Optometrist's Story

Maris arrived back in Estonia after years living across Europe. Her career path took her through optometry training and time working in eyewear retail — which gave her a unique perspective on vision correction. Back then, she saw glasses as the only sensible solution to refractive errors.

But as she moved away from optometry into finance, her thinking shifted. Living with glasses and contact lenses day-to-day brought frustrations she'd never fully considered before.

## The Real Cost of Glasses

Sports proved the biggest annoyance. "Before and after every training session, I had to go through the whole lens ritual," Maris explains. Weather made it worse too. In cold, rainy weather, her lenses fogged up constantly. And when she lived in Italy, even the best frames slipped down her nose in the heat.

Then came the packing chaos. "Endless juggling between prescription sunglasses and regular glasses. On budget airline trips, I was stuffing half my suitcase with eyewear frames." European carriers keep shrinking hand luggage limits. For Maris, it was irrational to carry that much optical baggage just to see clearly.

Years earlier, she'd worked as a waitress in a medieval-themed Tallinn restaurant — a job that required contact lenses for authenticity. "The Middle Ages didn't have glasses, obviously," she laughs now at the absurdity of that workplace rule.

## The Decision

When Maris finally considered [Flow3 laser eye surgery](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/), the choice felt obvious. Friends had already made the journey at KSA Silmakeskus with excellent results. "Given how technology has advanced and with trusted recommendations from people I knew, there was no reason to hesitate. I just needed to find the right moment."

## Procedure Day

Maris approached her [Flow3 procedure](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) like any other work day. The consultation beforehand was thorough enough to build genuine confidence — no room for doubt. "Walking into the procedure room, there was a moment of nerves," she admits. "But the team controlled the situation beautifully and made me feel completely supported."

## Recovery and Results

Recovery unfolded exactly as explained. The first week brought some discomfort — to be expected after any clinical intervention. Maris took two weeks off work, giving herself proper time to heal.

Three months later: "I see better than I ever did with glasses. I can't imagine living any other way now." What pleased her most? No more anxiety about medications, follow-up appointments, or aftercare complications. Just clear vision.

For someone who spent her career in eyewear, Maris found her answer in a different direction entirely.
