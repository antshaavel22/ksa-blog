## About Oskar

Oskar is an actor at Viljandi Ugala Theatre. He's someone who fills his life with things that matter—snowboarding, hiking in nature, solving crosswords, baking bread. He cycles, plays board games with friends, walks his dog. Life is full of discovery. And now, with perfect vision, he's seeing it all more clearly than ever.

## 15 Years of Glasses and Contacts—Enough

Oskar got his first pair of glasses in primary school. Sitting at the back of the classroom, the board became a blur. Soon after, when he started playing football seriously, he switched to contact lenses.

That decision meant 15 years of everything contact lens life involves: solution bottles, forgotten glasses, the occasional panic when a lens popped out mid-day with no spare. It became routine. Tedious routine. The kind you eventually want out of.

By his early thirties, the question wasn't hypothetical anymore. Why keep managing lenses and glasses if there was another way?

## Why Laser Surgery?

The thought had been sitting in the back of his mind for a while. But at 30, it became serious. "I did the maths," Oskar says with a smile. "If I get another 40-50 years of clear vision without all this—lenses, solutions, backup glasses, daily hassle—that's worth it. I don't want my gravestone to read: 'Oskar Punga—lens wearer. Glasses, backup glasses, daily lenses, monthly lenses, solution, case, blue L, white R.'" 

When friends told him about their positive experiences at KSA Silmakeskus, the decision felt straightforward.

## Recovery and First Impressions

On procedure day, there was a moment of mild discomfort—which Oskar expected. What surprised him was what came next.

By the next morning, he felt better than friends had warned. They'd told him the hard days were still coming. They weren't. Everything just kept improving. He was back at theatre rehearsal two days later, running a new production. Within three weeks, he was on stage in front of an audience with sharper vision than he'd ever known—just being sensible around the bright stage lights.

## Life After the Procedure

Details matter more now. Everything feels sharper. He finds himself noticing small things that used to blur into the background.

He loves looking out his window at Viljandi lake—it's clearer, lovelier somehow. At the theatre, watching performances is different. And one of his favorite moments: spotting a friend from across the room and waving first, before they see him. (Though occasionally he waves at a stranger. At least now he can't blame his eyesight.)

## What's Next?

Oskar admits he wasn't always the most careful lens wearer. He wore them too long, sometimes in situations where he probably shouldn't have. That's exactly why [Flow3](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) made sense for him. No daily ritual. No maintenance. No compromises.
