## We All Dream

People dream constantly—and about different things. Some fantasise about exotic travel, others about career advancement, a new car, finishing university. Many dream of having children, finding love, or simply having perfect vision.

But how do we actually make dreams come true?

## The New Year Question

The turn of the year is always a time for reflection. We make promises to ourselves. We set goals. We look back and ask the hard questions:

- What were your biggest wins this year?
- What did you learn?
- What are you most proud of?
- Which dreams did you actually achieve?

Many discover, with disappointment, that they haven't achieved a single one. John Lennon captured it perfectly: "Life is what happens when you're busy making other plans." Our dreams get buried under the daily grind. They slip away.

## Three Steps to Make Dreams Happen

Business coaches and goal-setting experts have developed systems for this. Here's the framework that works:

### 1. Define your goals with precision

Be specific. Write down exactly what you want—not vaguely, but clearly. The more precise you are about your desires, the easier they are to achieve. Put it all on paper: every dream, every wish, every goal. Be as detailed as possible.

### 2. Set a timeline

Wishing for something "sometime in the future" doesn't work. Assign each goal a date. Finish university in spring. Take that Scandinavian road trip in summer. Start your cooking course in September. Get a fresh start before Christmas.

If you want sharp vision for that summer trip, start the process six to eight weeks before you leave. Give yourself a deadline.

### 3. Take daily action

A great life is made of great days. Big dreams require consistent, daily effort. If you want to lose weight in three months, you don't do it once—you do small things every single day that support the bigger goal.

Look at your goals now. What can you do today, this week, this month to move toward them?
