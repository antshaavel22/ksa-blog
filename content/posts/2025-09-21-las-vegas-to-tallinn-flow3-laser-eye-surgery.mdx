Tatiana and Konstantin actually did it. They booked flights from Las Vegas to Tallinn, reserved a hotel for a week, and travelled across an ocean—all for laser eye surgery.

Why? That's the question we asked them too.

It turns out that when your partner has already had the procedure done at KSA Silmakeskus years ago and still has perfect vision, the choice becomes easier. But there's more to their story than that.

## The US Option Didn't Feel Right

When Tatiana needed to address her myopia, the obvious first thought was: do it in America. After all, they live there. But as Konstantin and Tatiana researched clinics back home, something didn't sit right.

US eye clinics, they discovered, primarily offer LASIK and Smile procedures—both of which involve creating a flap or incision in the cornea. For someone like Tatiana, who wasn't enthusiastic about that approach, it felt limiting.

Then Konstantin told her about [Flow3](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/)—a flapless surface laser treatment he'd undergone at KSA Silmakeskus back in 2014. No cut. No flap. Just precise laser reshaping of the corneal surface. Recovery takes roughly a week. And all these years later, his vision remains sharp.

Tatiana was intrigued. Here was a procedure type that wasn't readily available to her in the States—and proof of its success was sitting right across the dinner table.

KSA Silmakeskus has been performing eye surgery for decades, led by Dr. Ants Haavel, a highly respected ophthalmologist with over 20 years of experience. The clinic has completed 55,000+ procedures. When we asked what sealed the deal, Tatiana and Konstantin pointed to two things:
