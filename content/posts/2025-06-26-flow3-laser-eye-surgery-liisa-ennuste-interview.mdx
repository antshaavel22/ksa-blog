## The Woman Behind the Procedure

In 2017, Liisa Ennuste walked into KSA Silmakeskus and walked out glasses-free. She's the kind of person you notice: ambitious, high-achieving, sharp, positive. For years she led Nike's brand and marketing in Estonia. Then she levelled up—now she directs Nike's marketing across the Middle East from Dubai.

Her work thrives on bold campaigns and creative storytelling. But outside the office, Liisa lives for movement. Sport, fitness, balance—she chases it all. In 2018 alone, she read 28 books, setting herself monthly targets like it was nothing. She's driven. When she decides something matters, she does it.

That drive is exactly why we asked her about [Flow3](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/). Here's what she told us.

## "Life as a glasses wearer was constant friction"

**What was life like before your procedure? In what situations did glasses or contact lenses get in your way?**

The contact lens solution. That was my enemy. Running out at random moments, hunting down bottles in every country and corner of the world. I became genuinely expert at locating lens solution—I could tell you which pharmacies carried it, which supermarkets had the weird brands. It was exhausting.

And there was something else. People thought I was aloof. Because I couldn't see far clearly, I wouldn't smile at strangers passing on the street. They had no idea why—they just saw someone unfriendly. That bothered me.

## "I just decided. One day."

**What finally pushed you to have the procedure?**

Honestly, there was no dramatic moment. Like most big life decisions, I just... made it. One day I thought: *enough*. No more glasses. No more solution bottles. I walked into KSA, had the [Flow3 assessment](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/), found out I was a candidate, and suddenly there was no going back.

## "I was terrified. Clockwork Orange terrified."

**You mentioned real fear before surgery. What exactly scared you?**

Two things. First, I was convinced they'd hold my eyes open with some horrifying device—like that scene from *Clockwork Orange*. That image was stuck in my head.

Second, I was panicked about staying still. The laser's working, and I absolutely cannot move. That seemed impossible. My anxiety just spiralled around that.

## "Fifteen seconds. That's all it takes."

**How did you get past the fear?**

The facts helped. I learned the laser only works for about 15 seconds per eye. Thirty seconds total. Done. That's it.

But honestly? I also realised I couldn't let fear win. Thousands of people have had this done. They're fine. They're all fine. So I thought: *don't be that person who gives up because she's scared.* I wasn't going to be.

## "Recovery was faster than I expected"

**Walk us through what happened after surgery. How was recovery?**

The first few days, my eyes felt gritty and sensitive. Not painful—just uncomfortable, like something was in there. I wore sunglasses indoors. Within a week, that was mostly gone.

What surprised me was how quickly everything snapped into focus. Not gradually. It felt almost sudden. I'd wake up and think: *I can actually see*. Without anything on my face.

The real test came when I moved to Dubai a few months later. Intense heat, dry air, dust, long days outdoors. If the procedure was going to fail, that would be when. It didn't. My vision stayed clear.

## "To anyone thinking about it: Stop waiting."

**What would you say to someone dreaming of the procedure but still hesitating?**

Stop. Making. Excuses.

If you've been assessed and you're suitable for [Flow3](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/), you've already cleared the biggest hurdle. The procedure is 30 seconds. Recovery is a week. And then you get your life back.

I'm not someone who makes snap decisions. I'm cautious. I research. But this was one of the best decisions I've made. Not because of some miracle—I'm just no longer dependent on glasses and bottles of solution. I'm not wondering if people think I'm rude because I can't see them properly.

Don't let fear write your story. Fear's not a good advisor. The data is. The thousands of happy patients are. Trust that.

## "I chose Flow3 because it's faster and safer for my lifestyle"

**Why Flow3 specifically? Why not other laser options?**

Because it doesn't cut a flap in your cornea. That mattered to me. I wasn't going to give up sport and be careful for months. [Flow3](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) uses a gentler surface approach—flapless. Recovery's quicker. And in a high-contact life—which mine is—you want the safest option.

I also trusted the clinic. The team at KSA chose Flow3 for *their own eyes*. If the surgeons operating on you have chosen the same procedure for themselves, you know it's the real deal. That's not marketing. That's proof.
