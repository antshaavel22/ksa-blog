Myopia — nearsightedness — is no longer just a schoolchild's concern. It's now one of the fastest-growing vision problems globally. But how exactly do screens reshape our eyes? Why does vision suddenly worsen in adulthood? And when should you seriously consider lasting solutions?

## The Scale of the Problem

The World Health Organization projects that by 2050, myopia will affect half of humanity — roughly 5 billion people. The rise has been staggering, especially in children: between 1990 and 2023, myopia prevalence nearly tripled. Yet this isn't a child-only issue. In Europe, about one in three young adults now has myopia.

## What's Actually Happening Inside Your Eye?

Myopia is fundamentally anatomical. Your eyeball becomes too long, which throws the focal point forward — landing in front of the retina instead of directly on it. The result: distant objects blur while near ones stay sharp.

"Many people think myopia is simply about needing glasses," say the optometrists at KSA Silmakeskus. "The truth is more serious. It's a physical change in eye structure that can have lasting health consequences." This isn't just about prescription strength. It's about the eye itself having already changed shape.

## Screens and Indoor Life: The Real Culprits

Why do eyes start elongating? Screen time is one of the clearest answers. A major study of over 335,000 people found that each extra hour of daily screen use raises myopia risk by roughly 21%. At four hours daily — nearly standard for office work now — your risk nearly doubles.

Natural daylight, meanwhile, acts as a shield. During the pandemic, when children moved indoors and screen time jumped from 2.1 to 5.6 hours per day, myopia progression accelerated noticeably. This tells us something critical: just 1–2 hours outside daily can meaningfully slow vision decline.

## The Adult Myopia Myth

Many believe vision stops worsening after age 20. It doesn't. Over 90% of adult-onset myopia develops between ages 18 and 30. Research shows that 14% of young adults with normal vision at 20 develop myopia by 28.

"Regular eye checks matter even when your eyes feel fine," the KSA team emphasises. "Progression can be gradual and slip past you unnoticed."

## The Serious Side of High Myopia

Rising prescriptions aren't just an inconvenience. High myopia — starting around −5 to −6 diopters — significantly increases the risk of serious complications including retinal detachment, glaucoma, and macular degeneration. These aren't minor concerns. They can threaten your long-term vision.
