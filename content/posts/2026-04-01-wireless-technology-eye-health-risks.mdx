## The Hidden Cost of Modern Convenience

Have you ever stopped to consider that the very technologies making modern life easier might be quietly working against your health?

Wireless networks, mobile phones, WiFi routers—they're everywhere. Yet few of us pause to ask: what is this constant electromagnetic exposure actually doing to our bodies? To our eyes?

Unfortunate truth: many of the health problems we now treat as inevitable—chronic fatigue, sleep disruption, eye discomfort, accelerated ageing, even early-onset cognitive decline—may be far more connected to our technological environment than we realise.

## Estonia's Radiation Standards: A Cautionary Tale

Here's a fact that should concern everyone living in Estonia: our national electromagnetic radiation limits are among the highest in the world.

Higher than every other European country.

To put it in perspective: Russia, China, Italy, and France have standards 100 times stricter than ours. Liechtenstein? 10,000 times stricter.

Let that sink in.

We live under exceptional radiation exposure—and we're paying the price in our health and our lives. That persistent discomfort in your eyes. That unexplained fatigue. The mobile mast near your home. The WiFi router in your bedroom. They may all be connected.

## What the Science Says

### The Scale of the Problem

According to Landsman: "Every three seconds, someone receives an Alzheimer's or dementia diagnosis. Alzheimer's disease is the sixth leading cause of death—it kills more people than breast cancer and prostate cancer combined. And Western medicine has no solution."

This is precisely why awareness matters. Understanding the risks. Taking protective measures. It can meaningfully improve your health and that of your loved ones.

### Who Is Professor Olle Johansson?

Johansson is an Associate Professor in the Department of Neuroscience at the Karolinska Institute and head of the Experimental Dermatology Unit. He's a world-leading authority on electromagnetic radiation and its health effects.

His credentials speak for themselves: over 600 published original articles, reviews, book chapters, position statements, and conference reports in general and applied neuroscience, epidemiology, and biophysiology. He's also a co-author of the landmark BioInitiative Report.

## What You Can Do

You don't have to be passive.

- Be aware of radiation sources in your home and workplace.
- Create distance where possible between yourself and wireless devices.
- Limit exposure, especially at night.
- If you have concerns about your health, speak with a healthcare professional.
