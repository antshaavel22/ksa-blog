## The Sweet Spot for Laser Surgery

[Flow3 laser eye surgery](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) works beautifully for people aged 20–40. But what happens when you're older? The answer is more nuanced than a simple yes or no.

"Our goal at KSA Silmakeskus is to improve people's quality of life," explains Dr Ants Haavel, our lead ophthalmologist. "As we age, recovery from any medical procedure takes longer. So if you're thinking about laser surgery after 40, you need to be honest about whether the benefit justifies the wait."

The strength of [Flow3](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) lies in two things: the speed of the procedure itself, and the quick recovery. Both mean you get back to sharp vision fast—and actually enjoy the result. But if recovery stretches longer, or if complications become more likely, KSA won't perform the procedure.

"The older you are, the fewer years you have left to enjoy clearer vision," Dr Haavel points out. "Recovery slows down, and within a few years, reading glasses will become necessary anyway. That's why we ask patients over 45 to think carefully before deciding."

## Reading Glasses Are Coming Either Way

In our climate zone, most people need reading glasses by age 44. Near the equator, it's 33. Further north in places like Norway, it's closer to 47.

So here's the simple logic: even if you ditch your distance glasses at 40+, reading glasses aren't far behind. You'll still need spectacles—just different ones.

"The eye's lens is like any muscle in your body," Dr Haavel explains. "If your body is stiff, your blood vessels are rigid, your lifestyle is stressful, and you lack flexibility—all of that shows up in your lens. It hardens faster, and reading glasses come sooner."

But there's a flip side. "If you're vital, you do eye exercises regularly, you live healthily, and you maintain a positive outlook, your lens can stay flexible and clear into your 50s. In those cases, laser surgery is absolutely worth it."

## There Are Exceptions

While most people over 45 aren't ideal candidates for laser surgery, exceptions exist—and they matter.

If your eye's lens is still elastic, clear, and supple—reflecting an active lifestyle—you could be a candidate. Or if you have genuine, pressing reasons to be free of glasses for even a few years.

"Some of our patients have jobs or hobbies that genuinely demand sharp distance vision," Dr Haavel says. "We've had people who are about to become grandparents and want to see clearly when they're swimming with their grandchildren. They need sharp vision without aids."

These exceptions are evaluated carefully. We talk with you, review your eye data, and ask the key question: will this procedure actually improve your quality of life? Because even a few years of clear vision can be worth it.

The decision isn't automatic after 40. But it's not impossible either. It depends on your eyes, your health, your lifestyle—and what you really need your vision to do.
