## What Is Celery, Really?

Celery belongs to the *Apiaceae* family and grows as a root vegetable. You'll find it in kitchens across the globe, from America and Ireland to Japan and Australia. Its origins likely trace back to the Mediterranean and North Africa—archaeologists have even found celery-like plants in the tomb of Tutankhamun.

In the kitchen, celery shows up most often in soups and salads. Many people eat it as a snack too: it fills you up without piling on calories, which is why it's popular with anyone watching their weight.

## The Nutrients Inside Every Stalk

Celery packs a surprising nutritional punch. It contains minerals like calcium, sodium, copper, magnesium, iron, zinc, and potassium. You'll also find healthy fats and vitamins—A, K, C, E, D, and the full B complex (thiamine, riboflavin, folate, B6, and B12). Add fibre to that list, and you start to understand why this humble vegetable matters for your health.

## 15+ Health Benefits That Matter for Your Eyes

### 1. **Lowers Cholesterol**

Eating celery regularly can reduce LDL cholesterol—the kind that clogs arteries. Compounds called phthalides in celery also boost bile production, which further helps lower cholesterol levels. Less cholesterol means fewer blockages in your blood vessels and better overall cardiovascular health. The fibre in celery helps sweep cholesterol out of your system, which keeps your heart and eyes better supplied with healthy blood flow.

### 2. **Detoxifies Your Body**

Celery works as an antioxidant, and nothing goes to waste—you can eat the seeds, roots, leaves, and stalks. Regular celery consumption has been linked to reduced risk of kidney, pancreatic, liver, and gallbladder disease. Why does this matter for your eyes? A clean, well-functioning body delivers better nutrition to every tissue, including the delicate structures in your eyes.

### 3. **Reduces Blood Pressure**

Celery contains phthalides—organic compounds that lower stress hormones in the blood. This allows blood vessels to relax and dilate, giving blood more room to circulate and bringing your blood pressure down. Potassium in celery does similar work. Lower blood pressure means less strain on your cardiovascular system and better oxygen delivery to your eyes, where it's critical for maintaining healthy retinal tissue.
