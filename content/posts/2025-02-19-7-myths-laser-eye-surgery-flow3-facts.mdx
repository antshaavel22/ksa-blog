Imagine waking up and seeing sharp, clear detail from the moment your eyes open—no glasses, no contacts. It's possible. Yet laser eye surgery still carries myths that create unnecessary worry. Today we're cutting through seven of the most persistent ones.

## Myth 1: Laser eye surgery results don't last

People often believe the benefits fade over time. They don't. Laser eye surgery permanently reshapes your cornea. That change is for life—it cannot be undone.

In rare cases, a small amount of refractive error can return. But this isn't the treatment failing. It's your eye aging naturally, the way it would have anyway. Your cornea, like every other part of your body, changes with time. That's not the surgery reversing—that's biology.

What matters: you've gained years of clear vision you wouldn't have had otherwise. And if your vision does shift decades later, options exist. ## Myth 2: You'll smell burning during the procedure

Yes, there's an odour. No, your eye isn't burning.

The lasers we use generate virtually no heat in the tissue. The smell comes from a chemical reaction—when the laser meets your cornea, it releases carbon atoms into the air. It's natural. It's harmless. It means the procedure is working exactly as it should.

## Myth 3: Laser eye surgery can cause blindness

This is the fear that stops people from booking. Let's be clear: it's not realistic.

Laser eye surgery only reshapes the surface of your eye. Serious complications are exceptionally rare. Yes, like any medical procedure, there are risks—but they're minimal. The most common issue is inflammation, and we take strict preventive measures here at KSA. Our last case of significant inflammation? Five years ago.

Follow your surgeon's aftercare instructions, and you'll manage any risk well. Check our [patient stories](https://www.ksa.ee/experiences) to see what real results look like.

## Myth 4: Cheaper clinics deliver the same results

This is one of the most dangerous myths in the field.

Price reflects expertise, equipment, and aftercare. A clinic offering laser eye surgery at half the price of others isn't being generous—something is being cut. Perhaps outdated technology. Perhaps less experienced surgeons. Perhaps minimal follow-up care.

Quality costs. This isn't snobbery; it's physics and medicine. Ask yourself: would you trust a budget airline with your spine surgery? Your eyes deserve the same thinking. ## Myth 5: Recovery takes months

With [Flow3](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/), it doesn't.

Flow3 is flapless surface laser. No cut, no flap. Your cornea heals naturally, like any other surface wound. Most patients see clearly within days. Full stabilisation takes about a week. You're back to normal life—sport, work, everything—faster than you'd expect.

Compare that to traditional LASIK, which involves creating a flap. Longer healing, more restrictions, higher risk in contact sports. Flow3 is simpler. Your cornea knows how to heal itself.

## Myth 6: You can't do sports after laser eye surgery

Wrong. Especially with Flow3.

Traditional LASIK flaps can shift during high-impact activity. Flow3 has no flap. Once healed, your eye is as robust as it ever was. Athletes—footballers, martial artists, swimmers—choose Flow3 for exactly this reason. No limitations. No compromise.

## Myth 7: Laser surgery is only for young people

Age isn't the barrier. Your cornea's health is.

We've performed successful procedures on patients from their 20s to their 60s. The question isn't how old you are—it's whether your cornea is stable and thick enough. A simple assessment tells you that.

If laser isn't suitable, we have [ICB lens replacement](https://ksa.ee/en/icb-time)—an alternative that works beautifully for older patients or those with thinner corneas.
