Many of our patients wonder what vision will actually be like after [Flow3 laser surgery](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/). To cut through the uncertainty, we asked Dr Ants Haavel — our founder and lead ophthalmologist with 20+ years of experience — to answer the most pressing questions.

## How Does Vision Change Right After the Procedure?

Most patients are amazed at how clear their vision is within minutes of leaving the laser. We often see tears and smiles when we ask them what they can see. But let's be honest: **in the first few days, vision clarity sits at around 50% of your final result.** That said, compare that to 20 minutes before the procedure — when you couldn't see well without glasses — and the difference is staggering. Many of our patients had vision of just 3–10% before coming in. The leap in quality is immediate and unmistakable.

## How Quickly Do You Reach Maximum Vision?

Every body heals at its own pace. Based on our experience with over 55,000 procedures, tissue refinement and nerve recalibration takes roughly three months. But here's the good news: **95% of patients feel their vision is already excellent by week three.** We've noticed that men and patients with lower prescriptions tend to stabilize faster, though everyone is different.

Recovery speed also depends on your general health, energy levels, and your environment — whether you're working outdoors, in air-conditioned offices, or living in low-humidity climates. We always advise our patients on how to support faster healing through simple lifestyle adjustments and wellness habits. We treat the whole person, not just the eyes. Good health is the foundation for great vision.

## Will You See Better Than With Glasses or Contact Lenses?

It depends on your prescription and lens quality, but typically **your vision in the first week won't quite match your best-corrected vision with glasses.** Vision clarity isn't fully locked in for 7–10 days, though it's good enough for most daily tasks. But once everything settles — usually by week 3–4 — our patients consistently report something remarkable: their vision feels like it has a **new dimension entirely. It's sharper, cleaner, and often several times better than what they ever experienced with correction.**

## Do Colours, Contrast, and Depth Perception Change?

Yes. Dramatically. Patients report that **everything improves: sharpness, contrast, detail, colour vibrancy, and overall world perception.** The enthusiasm when they describe this transformation is impossible to miss. Their faces light up. It's powerful to witness people discovering how much they were actually missing.

At KSA Silmakeskus, we focus on correcting refractive error and optical focus. Everything else comes as a bonus. Honestly, the human body's ability to recalibrate itself — when given the chance — is remarkable.

## What About Night Vision?

Excellent question. Back in the mid-20th century, when refractive surgery was just beginning and procedures relied on incisions (like RK — radial keratotomy), most patients reported compromised night vision. Modern laser technology, especially [Flow3's flapless surface approach](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/), has virtually eliminated those issues. **Night vision typically improves or remains stable.** The precision of contemporary lasers means we preserve the optical integrity of your cornea far better than older techniques ever could.
