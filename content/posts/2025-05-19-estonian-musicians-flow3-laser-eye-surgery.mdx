## When vision matters on stage

If KSA Silmakeskus had its own Walk of Fame, it would be crowded. Over 55,000+ procedures, we've earned the trust of Estonia's brightest talent—actors, singers, athletes, public figures. Today, we're focusing on the musicians who chose [Flow3 laser eye surgery](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) and why clear vision changed everything.

For performers, the stakes are simple: good eyesight isn't optional. Dim stage lighting, sheet music in small print, reading a conductor's cue, glasses slipping mid-performance—myopia becomes more than a nuisance. It becomes a professional liability.

## Laura Põldvere: When you can't see the cue

Singer and musician **Laura Põldvere** underwent laser correction years ago. She remembers a live broadcast vividly.

"It was a major concert during Estonian Music Days—aired live on Classical Radio. The moment we started, someone on the other side of the auditorium was supposed to signal us with a hand gesture. I couldn't see them. I was terrified I'd come in early. You can't restart a 20-minute piece on live radio."

The solution? A band member holding up a white sheet of paper and waving it in the dark. "I'd see something white in the distance and *hope* that meant it was time," Laura recalls. "I was never quite sure."

Now, nearly 10 years after her procedure, those moments are gone. "The world is even more beautiful than before," she says simply.

## Rene Puura: Freedom on stage

**Rene Puura**, a professional keyboardist who's played in Karl-Erik Taukar's band, Terminaator, and Uku Suviste's group, has enjoyed clear vision for nearly 4 years thanks to [Flow3](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/).

His problem was physical. "During intense rock performances, my glasses would slip. My bandmate would have to stop mid-song and hand them back to me in the middle of a live set."

But it went deeper than that. Even offstage, he disliked how he looked in glasses—despite what others thought. "People said they suited my image. Maybe they did. But I didn't like them."

Now? "My new vision is like 4K combined with 3D cinema," Rene says. "Sometimes I catch myself staring at something, forgetting how clear it is. I just think: 'I'm seeing really well right now.'"

## Artjom Savitski: Connection with the audience

Singer **Artjom Savitski** faced a different challenge. His demanding schedule meant he couldn't memorise every lyric for every song. Sheet music was essential—which meant glasses were non-negotiable.

"I'd often forget them before going on stage. Huge stress. Real discomfort," he explains.

After his laser procedure, the stress vanished. But something unexpected happened too.

"I can now see the audience—actually see them. I can read their emotions. As a performer, that makes me ten times happier," Artjom says.

Clear vision didn't just fix his technical problem. It reconnected him with why he performs in the first place.

## Why Flow3 for performers

The [Flow3 procedure](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) is flapless—no corneal cut, no flap. That matters for athletes and performers who need their eyes working at full capacity quickly. Recovery is roughly one week. And because the cornea stays more intact, it's more resilient under physical stress.

Our entire clinical team chose Flow3 for their own vision. When the surgeons who perform the procedure trust it enough for themselves, that says everything.
