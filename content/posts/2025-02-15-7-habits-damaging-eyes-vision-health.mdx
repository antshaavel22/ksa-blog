Vision is one of our most precious senses—yet we damage it almost without noticing. Too much screen time, poor nutrition, and skipped eye exams can lead to lasting vision problems.

The good news? Small shifts in daily habits can make a real difference.

## 1. Spending too much time on screens

We all spend hours in front of computers, phones, and tablets. The strain of focusing on a close screen causes what's known as digital eye strain: dryness, blurred vision, headaches, and neck tension.

Try the 20–20–20 rule: every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away (about 6 metres) for 20 seconds. Your eyes will thank you.

## 2. Leaving sunglasses at home

UV radiation harms your eyes even on cloudy days. Without proper protection, it increases your risk of cataracts and age-related macular degeneration.

Invest in quality sunglasses that block at least 95% of UVA and UVB rays. Wear them year-round.

## 3. Rubbing your eyes

It seems harmless, but constant eye rubbing can damage the cornea and lead to keratoconus—a condition where the cornea thins and warps, seriously blurring your vision.

If your eyes itch, use a cool compress or lubricating drops instead.

## 4. Sleeping in contact lenses

Unless you're wearing 24/7 extended-wear lenses, sleeping in regular contacts dramatically increases your risk of eye inflammation and corneal damage.

Simple rule: always remove your lenses before bed, no matter how tired you are.

## 5. Poor nutrition

Your vision depends directly on what you eat. Lack of vitamins A, C, and E—along with lutein, zinc, and omega-3 fatty acids—accelerates vision decline.

Add these to your diet: leafy greens, carrots, citrus fruits, berries, flaxseed, and algae oil. They all support eye health.

## 6. Skipping regular eye tests

Many serious eye conditions—glaucoma, retinal changes—develop silently, with no obvious symptoms. By the time you notice something, it may be too late.

Get your eyes checked at least every two years, especially if you work at a screen or have a family history of eye disease.

## 7. Smoking (including secondhand smoke)

Smoking raises your risk of cataracts, retinal degeneration, and optic nerve damage. Even secondhand smoke can cause dry eye syndrome.

Do yourself—and your eyes—a favour. The benefits of quitting show up in just weeks.
