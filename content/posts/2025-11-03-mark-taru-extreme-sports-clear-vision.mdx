## The Ice Cross Dream Begins

Young Estonian ice cross racer Mark Taru thrives on adrenaline and speed. What started as a casual memory of watching Red Bull Crashed Ice on television became a serious passion—and now, a competitive career.

After leaving ice hockey, Mark remembered that old broadcast from childhood. He did his research, found qualification trials in Basel, Switzerland, and claimed second place. That result alone opened the door to the World Championships in Lausanne. With support from Red Bull Estonia, he's now one of Estonia's rising stars in ice cross racing.

His ultimate dream? A world championship title.

## Speed, Adrenaline, and Freedom

What draws Mark most to ice cross is the pure rush—racing down an icy course at 60km/h in front of thousands of spectators, battling competitors at full throttle.

Yes, it's risky. Yes, it's dangerous. And yes, that's exactly what he loves about it.

But Mark approaches the sport with the right mindset: stay positive, focus on the track, and embrace every moment. That mental discipline keeps him sharp when milliseconds matter.

## Why Crystal-Clear Vision Changes Everything

In ice cross, two things separate winners from the rest: raw speed and intimate track awareness. Mark trains year-round to maintain peak physical condition. But there's something equally critical—something many overlook.

"If my vision wasn't perfect, I honestly wouldn't feel safe out there," Mark explains. "I need to see every detail on the track to hit the fastest line possible. One small detail missed could cost you the race—or worse."

Clear vision isn't optional in extreme sports. It's foundational.

For athletes like Mark, even mild vision problems create hesitation. And hesitation at 60km/h can mean the difference between a medal and a crash.
