## Laura Pöldvere: When You Can't See the Signal

Singer and musician Laura Pöldvere underwent laser eye correction at KSA several years ago. She still recalls the anxiety of performing with myopia.

"During the Eesti Muusika Päevad festival, we had a major concert being broadcast live on Klassikaraadio," she remembers. "When we went live, we had to start immediately. But there was a person at the far end of the hall who was supposed to give me the signal to begin. The problem? I couldn't see him. I was terrified I'd start singing too early—and you can't restart a 20-minute piece on live radio. We found a workaround: he held up a large white sheet of paper and waved it. When I saw something white at the end of the hall, I guessed it was time to start. But I was never truly sure."

Since her laser procedure nearly a decade ago, those moments of doubt have vanished. Laura says the world looks even more beautiful now—and she never misses a cue.

## René Puura: Freedom from the Glasses Problem

Professional keyboardist René Puura—who has performed with Karl-Erik Taukar's band, Terminaator, and Uku Suviste's group—has enjoyed crystal-clear vision for nearly four years following his [Flow3 procedure](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/).

On stage, his glasses were a constant hazard. During energetic performances, they'd slip down, forcing a bandmate to pause mid-song and hand them back to him—mid-show. René also admits he disliked how he looked in frames, even if others saw them as part of his image.

"Now I see like a 4K 3D film," he says. "Sometimes I just stop and stare at something, thinking: I can actually see this incredibly well."

## Artem Savitski: No More Offstage Anxiety

Singer Artem Savitski faced a similar challenge. With a packed schedule and extensive repertoire, he couldn't always memorise every lyric perfectly. That meant sheet music stands on stage—and glasses were always essential. More than once, he'd left them in the wings, triggering real stress and disruption.

After laser vision correction, that anxiety disappeared. No forgotten glasses. No frantic searching backstage. Just clear sight and confidence.
