While comparing surgical methods is vital, it is equally crucial to contextualize the safety of laser vision correction against the most common daily alternative: contact lenses
. Many people mistakenly believe that avoiding surgery and wearing contacts is the safest choice, but clinical data proves otherwise
.
Contact lenses sit directly on the cornea, chronically reducing its oxygen supply—a condition known as hypoxia—and constantly introducing foreign bacteria to the ocular surface
. Over decades of daily wear, the cumulative risk of suffering a severe, vision-threatening eye infection (such as microbial keratitis) increases drastically
. In fact, the risk of developing a serious infection from wearing contact lenses is 300% (3 times) higher than the risk of infection from a laser eye procedure
.
Beyond safety, the lifetime financial burden is shocking. When factoring in the daily hassle, replacement lenses, cleaning solutions, and specialized eye drops, the lifetime cost of contact lenses averages around 18,000 euros
. When combined with the high rate of dry eye discomfort that lens wearers suffer, a one-time, flapless surface ablation procedure offers a safer, more permanent, and significantly more economical solution for true visual freedom
.
Conclusion The US military’s strong preference for surface ablation is deeply rooted in biomechanical science and rigorous, long-term safety data
. By completely eliminating the need for a corneal flap, procedures like Flow3 and SmartSurface offer the absolute highest degree of structural stability, a 4.5-fold lower risk of corneal ectasia compared to LASIK, and exceptional long-term visual clarity
. Powered by advanced SmartPulse laser technology that rapidly speeds up healing and maximizes patient comfort, this elite standard of vision correction is no longer reserved just for fighter pilots—it is the optimal choice for anyone who refuses to compromise on the safety, strength, and financial well-being of their eyes
