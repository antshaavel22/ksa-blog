## Myopia: Small on Paper, Big in Life

A prescription of –1.75 doesn't sound serious. On paper, it's hardly worth mentioning. But when you can't read road signs while driving, when the cinema screen blurs halfway through the film, when you fail to recognize a friend across the street—that's when a "small" minus becomes an everyday problem.

This is Anni's story. A mother of two. A healthcare professional. And someone who finally decided that her vision—and her freedom—were worth reclaiming.

## Finding Healthcare, Finding Her Path

In early 2024, Anni enrolled in nursing studies at Tallinn Health Care College. The opportunity to work at KSA Eye Center came through her studies, and she took it without hesitation.

Before this, her career had wandered through banking and construction—office work, routine, screen time. Years behind a desk had worn her eyes out. But it wasn't until she arrived at KSA that Anni understood the real issue: **screen fatigue was one problem, but her vision itself needed correction.**

She'd worn minus glasses since 10th grade. Never tried contacts. The prescription was modest, yet enough to limit her world:

- Road signs remained unclear until she was dangerously close
- Cinema trips meant missing fine details on screen
- Spotting acquaintances in public became a guessing game

She still laughs about one restaurant visit when she'd left her glasses at home. A blurry conversation with the waiter that felt mortifying then but feels absurdly funny now. That was her life, though—small embarrassments, constant small adjustments, always working around her vision rather than through it.

## Trust Built on Evidence

Working at KSA, Anni witnessed something powerful: patients returning with clear vision, with lives noticeably improved. She heard colleagues talk about their own [Flow3 procedures](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/)—not as marketing, but as genuine relief.

Trust didn't arrive all at once. It accumulated through observation. Through stories. Through results she could see with her own eyes.

"I regret not doing this sooner," she says now.

The decision crystallized when an optometrist colleague showed her exactly what uncorrected vision would feel like—a concrete, visual proof of what she stood to gain. Not a promise. An experience.

## The Day of the Procedure

Anni scheduled her [Flow3 treatment](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) for the evening, which meant she could work her regular shift beforehand. The procedure itself felt straightforward. No surprises. No anxiety. She knew what to expect and trusted the team completely.

The real moment came days later, driving home. She saw the road signs clearly—the ones that had haunted her for years. She could read them. Without glasses. From a distance.

She pulled over for a moment, just to absorb it.

## A Week Later, a Different Life

The recovery is fast with Flow3—around one week before vision stabilizes. Within days, Anni was noticing changes:

- No more fogged-up glasses in winter
- No more frantically searching for her specs
- No more hesitation before recognizing faces across a room
- No more cinema screens that blur halfway through

As a busy mother working in healthcare, these aren't luxuries. They're freedom. Real, everyday freedom.
