## What Happens to Your Vision Years After Laser Surgery?

**Can your eyes stay stable for 10, even 20 years after laser correction? Or does vision change over time?**

We've discussed this with Dr. Ants Haavel, our lead surgeon. But the real story? It comes from our patients — people who've lived with their results for nearly a decade or more.

Kristina Koppel had [Flow3 laser eye surgery](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) almost nine years ago. She's a copywriter by trade, works with text and content across different projects, and does the same in her spare time because she loves it. Crossfit training is non-negotiable in her week. She also bakes, creates desserts, and spends time outdoors whenever she can.

We asked her to look back on her procedure.

## "My vision hasn't changed in 9 years"

**Is your vision today as good as it was right after your laser surgery in early 2014?**

Yes. My vision has stayed exactly as good as it was immediately after the procedure. At my occupational health check-ups, my visual acuity is flawless. I can always read the smallest line.

**Have you had any vision problems in all these years? Anything that needed fixing?**

No. Not once in nine years.

**Do you regret having the surgery? Would you recommend it?**

I'm extremely happy with it. Honestly, having the laser surgery was one of the best decisions of my life. My quality of life changed dramatically.

## The Journey: From Sceptic to Advocate

When I first thought about eye surgery, it seemed impossible — completely out of reach. After 10 years wearing contact lenses, my eyes stopped tolerating them. They became extremely sensitive. I genuinely believed the lenses had damaged my eyes permanently, and that laser surgery wouldn't even be an option for me.

I was wrong.

When I came in for a consultation at KSA, they explained I had no contraindications. My fears were unfounded. So I went ahead. And I'd do it again in a heartbeat. If you're worried, still come in for a consultation — this procedure genuinely changes your life.

## Daily Life: Then and Now

Before surgery, I wore glasses with a prescription of –4.57 in one eye and –5 in the other. Everything changed after that.

Waking up and actually *seeing* clearly — no scramble for glasses as the first thing in the morning. Sport became genuinely enjoyable. No discomfort from frames or the fear of lenses falling out at the pool or sauna. I can even recognise people I know without squinting.

It's freedom, honestly. The small moments matter.
