## An Ancient Remedy, Scientifically Proven

Peppermint isn't new. Far from it.

The leaves have been found in Egyptian pyramids dating back to 1000 BC. Cleopatra knew about it. So did the Greeks and Romans. For thousands of years, peppermint was *the* medicinal plant—trusted by healers across the ancient world.

But here's what changed: modern science finally caught up.

Over the past two decades, rigorous research has revealed what our ancestors already knew—peppermint is genuinely remarkable. It has more than 15 documented health benefits. And yes, several of them relate directly to eye health and inflammation control, including surprising effects against herpes simplex virus.

Most of us know peppermint as a flavoring or a warming tea. We don't realize what we're actually holding in our hands.

## Why Peppermint Matters for Your Health

The evidence is substantial. Let's walk through some of the key findings.

### Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS)

Since the late 1990s, researchers have documented that enteric-coated peppermint oil capsules are safe and effective for IBS—one of the fastest-growing digestive disorders.

In one study involving children, **75% of patients taking peppermint oil experienced significant pain relief within just two weeks.**

A 2005 trial in adults concluded: "Given the limited treatment options currently available for IBS, peppermint oil (1–2 capsules daily for 24 weeks) appears to be a primary treatment option for IBS patients with mild-to-moderate constipation or diarrhea, helping reduce overall symptoms and improve quality of life."

Another 2007 study showed an even more striking result: **a 50% reduction in overall IBS severity scores** among patients using peppermint oil.

More recent research from January has continued to confirm these benefits, with peppermint oil proving consistently effective at reducing IBS-related symptoms.

## Eye Health & Inflammation

Beyond digestion, peppermint has documented anti-inflammatory properties. This matters for anyone concerned with eye health—whether you're recovering from [Flow3 laser eye surgery](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) or managing chronic eye irritation.

The herb's active compounds help control inflammatory responses and have shown promise against herpes simplex virus, which can cause ocular inflammation and corneal complications.
