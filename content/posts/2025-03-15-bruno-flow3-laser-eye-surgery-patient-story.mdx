You might remember Bruno from our social media post — the one with the pink-taped glasses. That image made people smile and sparked conversations with others who'd faced similar frustrations. Now, a few months on from his procedure, we caught up with him to hear the full story: how he found KSA, and how recovery has gone.

## The everyday life of a glasses wearer

Bruno is what you'd call a regular Estonian guy. He's from Viljandi, a small town he speaks about warmly, where he works as a project manager at Viljandi Metall. His job involves overseeing the manufacture of carbon steel structures — managing timelines, quality, and budgets across projects of varying complexity.

Outside work, Bruno's life revolves around family and home. With two daughters (the youngest just two years old), downtime fills up fast. While he jokes he doesn't have hobbies, we'd argue otherwise. He's clearly got a technical mind — summers find him tinkering with machinery on his land. But his real passion? Volleyball. He's been playing since school, competing regularly in amateur tournaments. Even now, he plays for Viljandi's club team in the national league. "It's worth mentioning too," Bruno adds, "that our company supports Viljandi women's volleyball."

## Twenty years in lenses

For the better part of two decades, Bruno has managed all these activities — and life itself — through glasses and contact lenses. The inconveniences stack up quickly. Sports with glasses? A nightmare. "They fall off, slip down your nose, get fogged up or dirty. Contacts help a bit, but beach volleyball is impossible — one grain of sand and you can't rub your eye without risking losing the lens," he explains.

Even everyday situations proved frustrating. Walking from cold into warmth, his glasses would fog instantly. They'd cloud over quickly in the sun. And prescription sunglasses? Impractical, though in a pinch (like driving), he'd layer them over his regular frames.

Then came the moment that crystallised everything: a few days before his procedure, Bruno was playing in a tournament. Because he needed to pause contact lenses for 10 days before surgery, he was stuck in glasses. A ball from an opposing player caught him in the face. His frames shattered, lenses shattered, and the broken frame cut his face. The game went on — a quick-thinking friend taped the glasses back together with pink tape at the sideline just so Bruno could finish the match.

## The breaking point

That moment summed it up. Twenty years of small defeats. Glasses breaking. Contacts failing at exactly the wrong moment. Missing out on seeing clearly during the activities he loved most. Bruno decided it was time for a different answer.

He chose [Flow3, the flapless surface laser procedure](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) at KSA Silmakeskus in Tallinn. The appeal was clear: no flap creation, faster healing, and suitable for someone with an active lifestyle. The entire clinical team at KSA — including the surgeon — have chosen Flow3 for their own vision. That level of confidence matters.

## Recovery and life after

A few months on, the difference is unmistakable. Bruno no longer needs glasses or contacts. He plays volleyball without worrying about his frames. Rain, sun, sand — none of it matters anymore. The recovery took about a week, as expected with Flow3, and he's back to normal life entirely.

When asked if he'd recommend it, the answer is immediate. For anyone who's spent years managing vision correction — squinting at screens, fussing with cases, missing moments because their glasses were smudged — Flow3 offers something simple: clarity. No equipment. No compromise. Just seeing.
