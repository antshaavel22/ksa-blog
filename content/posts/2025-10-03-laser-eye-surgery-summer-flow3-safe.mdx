## Can You Have Laser Eye Surgery in Summer?

We get asked this question regularly. The short answer: yes, absolutely.

Historically, summer eye surgery was risky. Without modern antibiotics, wounds didn't heal well in heat. But that was then.

"Today's medicine and technology have changed everything," explains Dr. Ants Haavel, founder and lead surgeon at KSA Silmakeskus. "We perform vision corrections safely in every season."

## Why Flow3 Changes the Game

The difference lies in how we operate. [Flow3 is not traditional laser surgery](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) — it's a flapless surface procedure. No blade. No incision.

"Because we don't cut into the cornea, healing is faster and complications are rare," Dr. Haavel notes. "That makes summer timing irrelevant."

Over 55,000 procedures completed. The entire clinical team chose Flow3 for their own eyes. That's the real proof.

## What About Climate?

Many countries perform these procedures year-round in hot climates. Results stay consistent. "And let's be honest," Dr. Haavel smiles, "Estonia's summers aren't exactly scorching."

The season simply doesn't affect outcomes.

## When Should You Actually Come In?

Here's Dr. Haavel's honest advice: "The best time is when you're ready. Not the calendar — your readiness."

That said, ideal candidates are typically between 18 and 45 years old, with stable vision and good overall health. Your eyes need to have finished developing, your prescription needs to be stable, and your general fitness should be solid.

Beyond that? Summer, winter, spring, autumn — it makes no difference.

If you've been putting off vision correction, don't let the season hold you back. [Book a consultation](https://ksa.ee/en/quick-test/) and find out if you're a candidate.
