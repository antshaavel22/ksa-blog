## Meet Taimi

Taimi is a restless world traveller, a passionate dancer, and an acro-yoga enthusiast. Her days blur together organizing community events. Her nights? Spinning to the rhythm of Zouk and Bachata on dance floors across the globe. But for seventeen years, one thing didn't fit the beat of her life: glasses and contact lenses.

She got her first pair of glasses in fifth grade—and hated them. Then came seventeen years of constant dependence on vision aids. On the dance floor, contacts were survival; glasses simply wouldn't stay on when the movement got serious. But at acro-yoga classes, in saunas, at the pool with friends, without her lenses she could barely see beyond a few metres. Then came the day at the water centre when she lost a lens mid-dive. "I laughed at myself," she says, "but it perfectly captured how exhausting the whole thing really was."

## How the Dream Became Real

She'd been dreaming about [laser eye surgery](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) for years. The decision came down to one thing: she wanted it done at home in Tallinn. No cross-country recovery journeys. No complicated logistics.

Then friends recommended KSA Silmakeskus. Not just for the expertise, but for something harder to measure—the human touch. Staff actually followed up afterwards. They cared.

What happened next moved her deeply. Three people became her anchor:

First, her Indian friend—who sold her own property to support Taimi's dream. Then her publisher best friend, who believed in the same values. And finally, her partner: for six months, he took on the full rental payments alone so she could save what she needed.

"The gratitude I feel is still enormous," she says.

## Recovery: Berries, Puzzles, and Songs

The first days after the [Flow3 procedure](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) were quiet. Her eyes needed rest—but that meant discovering other senses. Blueberries burst on her tongue. Raspberries melted. Friends came over. They sang folk songs, worked on puzzles, ate pancakes together.

At first, Taimi caught herself reaching to remove her "glasses" before bed. Pure muscle memory from seventeen years. Now, that moment makes her smile.

She dances harder. She sees further. She swims without hesitation. No more losing lenses in pools. No more squinting across rooms. No more compromises.
