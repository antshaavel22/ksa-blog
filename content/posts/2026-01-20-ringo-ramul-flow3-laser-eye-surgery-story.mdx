## A 30th Birthday Gift to Himself

Ringo Ramul is one of Estonia's most celebrated theatre actors and directors. After years of considering laser eye surgery, he made the decision that would change his daily life: he had his eyes corrected at KSA Silmakeskus.

For decades, glasses had been part of Ringo's identity. But on his 30th birthday, he decided it was time for a change—to give himself the gift of clear vision and the freedom that comes with it.

"I told myself: that's enough. Time to finally do this for myself," he says.

## When Myopia Became Funny (and Awkward)

Wearing glasses had its moments. Myopia meant that whenever Ringo went swimming, to the sauna, or anywhere without his glasses, something amusing—and often embarrassing—would happen.

He remembers swimming up to someone he thought was his partner, only to realize at the last second it was a complete stranger. In the sauna, he'd strike up conversations with people he mistook for friends, only to be met with confused silence.

"Without my glasses at a spa or pool, I could pretty much guarantee something funny would happen," he laughs.

Though he'd adapted to it over the years, the inconvenience never really went away.

## The Tipping Point

The final push came from those around him. Friends and family who'd already had the procedure at KSA Silmakeskus were enthusiastic. They'd all had great experiences and encouraged Ringo to take the step.

"Glasses had become part of who I am," Ringo explains. "I kept thinking about the procedure but kept putting it off for no real reason. Then so many people I knew recommended KSA—they couldn't speak highly enough of it. In the end, I followed their lead."

## The [Flow3 Procedure](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) Itself

## Life After

Today, Ringo enjoys the simple freedom most people take for granted: waking up and seeing clearly without reaching for glasses. He can move through his day—on stage, off stage, in the pool—without the constant adjustment and worry.

"I've realised my dream of enjoying those simple moments with clear vision," he says. "It's given me back a kind of freedom and peace of mind I didn't fully expect."

For a professional actor whose craft demands presence, confidence, and complete trust in himself, that clarity—both literal and metaphorical—makes all the difference.
