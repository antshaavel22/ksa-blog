## Your Wedding Day Deserves Perfect Vision

Every year, people come to KSA Silmakeskus with the same goal: clear vision for their wedding day. We love it. There's something genuinely rewarding about a client returning months later—with a new name in their email signature—to tell us how much they enjoyed seeing everything sharply and clearly on the most important day of their life.

"Vision correction is surprisingly common among brides," says Liisi Toom, optometrist at KSA Silmakeskus. "And it makes complete sense."

## Why Clear Vision Matters on Your Wedding Day

Your wedding is not the day to worry about small details. If you wear glasses, you're thinking about them—whether they stay on, how they look in photos, how the reflection catches the light. If you wear contact lenses and haven't worn them regularly, there's stress: Will they stay in place? Will happy tears wash them out? Will they irritate your eyes and ruin your makeup?

"Confidence matters," Toom explains. "A bride feels better when she's not managing glasses or worrying about lenses. And photographically, glasses and wedding dresses don't always work together. Even subtle lens distortion can complicate good photos."

Both brides and grooms come for this reason. Some couples even come together—which, in Toom's words, is "particularly lovely."

## Timeline: When to Have Your Procedure

Ideally, have your [Flow3 procedure](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) at least **one month before your wedding**.

Why? Your vision stabilises and sharpens within that window—enough to enjoy freedom and take in every moment without compromise. You can also return to active life, sports, and dancing by day seven.

If you've worn contact lenses before and want to switch to lenses for the day, at least one month of regular wear beforehand will help you feel confident.

## How the Flow3 Procedure Works

First, we run a thorough eye examination to establish whether you're a suitable candidate. This is important—not everyone is, and we'll be honest about it.

You'll spend roughly an hour at our Tallinn clinic on procedure day. The actual laser work takes only **10–15 minutes**.

The [Flow3 procedure](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) is flapless—we correct the cornea surface directly, with no cut and no flap created. This makes it safer for active people and athletes, and recovery is faster than traditional methods.

## Who Can Have the Procedure?

The ideal age range is 20–40 years. Your vision must also be stable—if your prescription has changed significantly in the past year, we'll ask you to wait a year or two.

Some people aren't suitable candidates. Occasionally, the cornea is too thin, or another health factor makes the procedure inadvisable. We'll know after your examination—so the first step is always a proper assessment.

## Not Suitable for Laser? We Have Another Option

If you're not a candidate for [Flow3 laser eye surgery](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/), we offer [ICB lens replacement](https://ksa.ee/en/icb-time)—a procedure that replaces your natural lens with an implant, giving you clear vision without the need for glasses or contacts.
