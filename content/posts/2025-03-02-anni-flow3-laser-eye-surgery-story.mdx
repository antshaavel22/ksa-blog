## Anni's Story: Why a KSA Staff Member Chose Flow3

Myopia doesn't sound serious on paper. Minus −1.75 doesn't seem like much. But when it stops you reading street signs, catches you squinting at cinema screens, and means you second-guess whether that's someone you know on the street—it becomes an inconvenience that shapes your daily life.

This is Anni's story. She's an operating nurse at KSA Silmakeskus, mother of two, and someone whose path to [laser eye surgery](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) changed both her vision and her quality of life.

## The Road to Medicine—and to KSA

Anni started training as a nurse at Tallinn Health Care College in early 2024. The opportunity to join KSA Silmakeskus came through her studies, and the decision to apply was almost immediate.

Before nursing, she'd worked across very different sectors—banking, then construction. Desk jobs. Routine. Long days staring at screens. At some point, she felt the pull toward something more meaningful. Her family had someone requiring ongoing medical care, and that experience deepened her interest in healthcare. **The eye strain from years in banking felt manageable. It wasn't until she started at KSA that Anni realised screen fatigue was only half the story—her vision itself needed clarity.**

She'd worn minus glasses since tenth grade. Never tried contact lenses. The prescription was mild, but it meant:

- Road signs weren't readable from a distance
- Cinema details got lost
- Recognising people on the street became a guessing game

She laughs remembering a restaurant visit when she'd forgotten her glasses and a blurry encounter with the chef. Awkward then. Just funny now.

## The Decision: Trust Built on Witnessing Results

Working at KSA, Anni saw [Flow3 results](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) firsthand. She heard colleagues' experiences. Day by day, her confidence grew.

"The only thing I regret," she says now, "is not doing it sooner."

On procedure day, she felt certain. She knew exactly what would happen. She trusted the team completely.

The final push came when a colleague—an optometrist—showed her exactly how clearly she'd see without glasses. Concrete. Clear. Real.

## Procedure Day: From Colleague to Patient

Everything went smoothly. Anni knew what to expect and felt in capable hands. Because she'd scheduled her procedure for the evening, she'd worked normally that day.

The moment that stayed with her arrived days later, driving home. She saw the road signs in the distance. Sharp. Clear. No glasses.

That was the instant the change became real.

## The First Week: Noticing What She'd Been Missing

Recovery after [Flow3 is fast](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/). Vision stabilises within days, not months. For Anni, the improvement was immediate.

She noticed things she'd taken for granted before:

- Recognition without hesitation when meeting people
- Cinema without effort
- Reading distant signs naturally
- Rain no longer meant fogged-up vision
- No more losing glasses, no more searching for them

"It's not just about seeing clearly," she reflects. "It's about not *thinking* about seeing. Vision just... works."
