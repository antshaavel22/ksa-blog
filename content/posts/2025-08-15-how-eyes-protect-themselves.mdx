## The Body's Built-in Defence System

The human body works like a finely tuned Swiss watch—different systems operating in perfect harmony to keep everything running smoothly. But here's where we have the edge over any timepiece: when something goes wrong, we don't always need a specialist. Our bodies have evolved powerful mechanisms to defend and repair themselves, often without us noticing a thing.

These protective systems are everywhere. Some are obvious—adrenaline flooding your system when you sense danger, preparing you to fight or flee. Others are invisible: a fever signals your immune system battling an infection. When you cut yourself, your blood clots automatically, sealing the wound and blocking harmful bacteria. Even a sneeze is a defence mechanism—your body's reflex to clear irritants from your nose. And yes, we instinctively squeeze our eyes shut while sneezing. That's protection too: preventing the force of the sneeze from damaging delicate eye muscles and tiny blood vessels.

**We are biologically programmed to protect our eyes.**

## Your Eyes' Inner Guard Systems

The human body is a sophisticated machine where every component has a specific, purposeful role. Your eyes are no exception.

Your cornea, lens, and vitreous automatically absorb ultraviolet radiation. When light is bright, your pupil contracts, protecting the sensitive retina from harmful glare. Your eyelashes and eyelids act like tiny bouncers, filtering out dust particles and debris floating in the air—think of them as windscreen wipers for your eyes, constantly clearing away whatever would block your view.

Your tear film is equally clever: it lubricates, protects against infection, and keeps the eye surface healthy. Your conjunctiva (the clear membrane covering the white of your eye) has immune cells standing guard. Your retina contains millions of light-sensitive cells working together to send visual information to your brain at breathtaking speed.
