## Why artists need perfect vision

For anyone on stage, good eyesight isn't a luxury. It's essential.

Dimly lit venues. Sheet music at arm's length. A conductor's cue from across the auditorium. Glasses slipping mid-performance. For performers with myopia, these aren't minor inconveniences—they're real obstacles to confidence and artistry.

Singer and musician **Laura Põldvere** knows this firsthand. Nearly ten years ago, she performed at Estonian Music Days with a live broadcast on Klassikaraadio. A colleague at the back of the hall was supposed to signal the start. But Laura couldn't see them. Panic set in. Would she start too early? In the end, they had to wave a large white sheet of paper just to get her attention. Even then, she wasn't certain.

Since her [Flow3 procedure](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/), that anxiety has vanished. Now she says the world looks even more beautiful than before.

**Rene Puura**, a professional keyboard player who's performed with Karl-Erik Taukar's band, Terminaator, and Uku Suviste's ensemble, spent years battling an embarrassing problem: his glasses would fall off during energetic live performances. A bandmate would have to pause, retrieve them, and hand them back.

Four years ago, he chose the [Flow3 procedure](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/). His verdict? His new vision feels like 4K and 3D cinema combined.

**Artjom Savitski**, another accomplished singer, used to carry his glasses everywhere for performances. Reading sheet music was impossible without them. Forgetting them caused real stress and frustration.

Now? That burden is gone.

"I can actually see the audience and their emotions," he says. "It makes me ten times happier as an artist."

## Beyond the stage: everyday life transformed

Professional success matters, but so does quality of life.

**Eda-Ines Etti**, a beloved singer, was a long-time contact lens wearer. Summer was always difficult—swimming, spontaneous outdoor activities, travel. Lenses became uncomfortable in heat and humidity. She had her eyes corrected at KSA nearly 16 years ago.

The difference? Her summers and travels are incomparably better now. No anxiety. No discomfort. Just clear vision whenever she needs it.

**Kadri Voorand**, a singer-songwriter and traveller, loves hiking and exploring alone. Before her Flow3 procedure five years ago, she constantly worried about losing a lens in the field with no way to see properly without help. That anxiety shaped her choices—limiting where she could go, what she could do.

After surgery, that fear evaporated.

"Now I can even go surfing alone without worrying I won't see the shore when I come out of the water," she says. "Life is so much easier. I wish I'd done it ten years earlier."
