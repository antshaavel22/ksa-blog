## Quick Answer

When selecting an eye clinic, focus on five core areas: **procedure method, laser technology, surgeon training, transparent pricing, and patient trust**.

## Why the Right Clinic Matters

Refractive surgery has become competitive—which makes the choice genuinely difficult. Your eyes are irreplaceable, and most people are rightfully careful about who they trust with their vision. That caution is wise. The good news is that a few clear criteria can guide you toward a clinic that deserves that trust.

Here are the five things we recommend investigating before you decide.

## 1. The Procedure Method

Start by understanding the different approaches available. Attend consultations and ask which methods suit *your* eyes specifically. Every eye is unique—what works for one person may not work for another.

When comparing methods, ask yourself: What matters more to you? Faster recovery, or long-term visual quality and lower risk? Are you an athlete or outdoors enthusiast? Some procedures offer better safety for active lifestyles than others.

For example, [Flow3 laser surgery](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) is flapless—no cut, no flap—which makes it inherently safer during contact sports and recovery takes about one week. For patients unsuitable for laser correction, [ICB lens replacement](https://ksa.ee/en/) is an excellent alternative that replaces your natural lens.

Don't assume all methods achieve the same visual outcome. They don't.

## 2. The Laser Technology

Yes, lasers do matter—despite what you might hear.

Consider this: saying "all lasers are the same" is like saying "all cars are the same." New cars are safer, more reliable, more efficient. The same principle applies to laser technology.

Ask your clinic: How new is the laser? What generation? What's its track record? If they don't answer clearly, that's a signal to keep looking.

You can also research the equipment independently online. Modern laser technology delivers better precision, faster treatment times, and fewer complications.

## 3. Ongoing Training and Expertise

Refractive surgery advances rapidly. New techniques emerge. Equipment evolves. Knowledge deepens. Experience grows.

At KSA Silmakeskus, our entire team—from reception staff to Dr. Ants Haavel—pursues continuous professional development to stay current with global standards. We hold ISO 9001 certification and maintain rigorous quality protocols.

Ask whether your clinic's surgeons attend international conferences, whether staff complete regular training, and whether the centre has formal quality certifications. Commitment to ongoing learning is a powerful trust signal.

## 4. Price Versus Quality

Price matters, but it should never be your only criterion.

Think of it this way: you can replace a car. You cannot redo your eyes (in most cases, you get one opportunity). Unusually cheap pricing often signals corners cut elsewhere. Ask yourself:

- How new and advanced is the laser they're using?
- Are medications included in the quoted price?
- What is their follow-up care policy? Do you pay extra?
- What happens if a second procedure is needed? At what cost?

Quality varies significantly between clinics. Your job is to understand what you're paying for, not just what you're paying.

Check our [pricing page](https://ksa.ee/en/price-list/) to see what transparency looks like.

## 5. Trust and Atmosphere

Visit multiple clinics in person. Speak to staff. Send emails. Notice how you *feel*.

- Do they answer your questions thoroughly?
- Do they feel rushed or attentive?
- Do they explain options clearly, or push one procedure?
- Is the clinic clean and modern?
- Does the surgeon seem confident but not arrogant?

Trust is built through communication, respect, and evidence. After 55,000+ procedures, KSA Silmakeskus has built that reputation—but every clinic is different. You need to feel confident handing your vision to whoever you choose.

## The Bottom Line

Your choice of eye clinic shapes your outcome. Take time. Ask questions. Compare. The clinic you choose should excel not just in equipment and method, but in communication, training, and genuine care for your long-term vision.

Your eyes deserve nothing less.
