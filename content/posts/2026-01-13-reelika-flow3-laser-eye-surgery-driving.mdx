On a bright summer's day, Reelika Tammeoru walked into KSA Silmakeskus and walked out glasses-free. The reason was refreshingly practical: she wanted to drive comfortably. Everything else that comes with clear vision—seeing the world without foggy lenses—was simply a bonus.

Reelika spent nearly 13 years in England. What started as a short trip became a life: university in London, a marketing degree, a 7-year career. Recently, she and her partner moved back to Estonia. That's when the real need for vision correction emerged.

## Foggy glasses in a moving car

Reelika had never been fond of glasses. For years, she squinted her way through life—especially awkward on holidays. The turning point came when she needed a driving license and a car. The timing was difficult: driving lessons with fogged-up glasses from face masks felt like learning to navigate with your eyes closed. Add rain, snow, and wet lenses in the Estonian seasons, and the frustration mounted.

But the deeper reason was simpler. She wanted to see. Really see. Reelika loves nature, and Estonian nature is genuinely beautiful. Why experience it through a smudged lens?

## Taking the leap

She booked her consultation at KSA but postponed the actual [Flow3 procedure](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) three times. The nervousness was real. One month before her driving test, she decided: enough deliberating. Time to commit.

On procedure day, Reelika admits she was slightly anxious—especially when selecting protective sunglasses at reception. But Dr. Ants Haavel knew how to calm her mind. She even learned something unexpected: her eye pigment is beautiful. Looking back, the procedure itself felt straightforward. Recovery was smooth. She credits Dr. Haavel's 20+ years of experience and the clinical team's expertise.

## Life after the procedure

Her partner notices the difference immediately—he can look her in the eyes now. Reelika enjoys unobstructed views of nature. Driving has become what it should be: a pleasure, not a frustration. And yes, no more buying new glasses every two years.

One month after her [Flow3 laser eye surgery](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/), Reelika passed her driving test. Her vision: A+.
