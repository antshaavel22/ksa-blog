## Early Glasses and Years of Compromise

Kristin describes herself as active—a busy mum, a believer in healthy living, and someone always on her feet. But her story to clear vision isn't straightforward. It took three attempts before the [Flow3 procedure](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) finally worked. What happened after changed everything. Today, she trains without glasses, earned her personal trainer certification, and lives the life she'd imagined for years.

Glasses came early for Kristin. By second grade, she was wearing them. At first, the adjustment was hard. But soon she understood: they helped her see. Over time, she tried contact lenses—they irritated her eyes and never felt right. By her twenties, glasses had become part of the problem, not the solution. They got in the way at the gym, fogged up during exercise, and felt limiting. There was one moment that crystallised it all: in the delivery room, holding her newborn, her prescription was so strong she was terrified to take her glasses off. Without them, she couldn't see her baby's face clearly.

## Recovery That Surprised Her

Friends had warned her: laser eye surgery recovery is brutal, it takes weeks, your eyes will be uncomfortable. None of that happened.

Within days, Kristin was back to normal life. The initial haziness cleared fast. She kept her eyes well-lubricated, wore sunglasses outdoors as advised, and felt surprisingly good. Most of what she'd heard about painful recovery simply didn't match her experience. [The Flow3 procedure](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) meant no corneal flap—just the surface was reshaped—and her body bounced back.
