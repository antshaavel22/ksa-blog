## Why Athletes Come to KSA

We've helped professional athletes across nearly every sport discipline ditch their glasses for good. The stakes are highest at elite levels. When you're stepping into a boxing ring, surfing big waves, or sprinting downhill on a mountain bike, you can't afford to lose a contact lens mid-performance. Wearing glasses in those conditions simply isn't realistic.

Poor eyesight isn't just inconvenient for athletes—it can be genuinely dangerous. Lose a contact lens and you lose not just your own safety, but potentially that of others around you. Blurred vision distorts your sense of balance, spatial awareness, and distance perception. Critical details slip past unnoticed.

## Why Athletes Choose Flow3

Many athletes select [Flow3 laser eye surgery](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) at KSA for one clear reason: it's flapless.

Unlike traditional methods, Flow3 involves no surgical cut and no flap. Instead, we gently refine the corneal surface by just a few micrometers. The change is so minimal that after just one week, you're cleared to return to intensive training—boxing sessions, swimming, even skydiving.

"Most of our patients are genuinely shocked," says Dr. Ants Haavel, our lead ophthalmologist. "The fact that they can get back to an active lifestyle within seven days surprises almost everyone."

For professional athletes, this matters enormously. Training schedules can't be derailed by lengthy recovery windows.

There's another crucial advantage: conventional flap-based surgery (like LASIK) requires strict physical restrictions for much longer. And here's something many people don't realise—the corneal flap created during those procedures never fully reattaches. You'll need to take that into account forever when choosing contact sports or future lifestyle decisions.

With Flow3, there are no lifelong restrictions. Your eye heals completely.

## Combat Athletes Love Flow3

Seven days. That's all it takes to return to sparring.

This is why [Flow3](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) has become the go-to choice for boxers, MMA fighters, and other combat athletes. One of Estonia's most recognisable figures, former sumo wrestler **Baruto** (Kaido Höövelson), corrected his myopia at KSA more than eight years ago.

For Baruto, poor eyesight had become a real problem. Years of squinting created tension, and stepping into the sumo ring without clear vision meant he couldn't lock eyes with his opponent with full confidence. His vision was always soft, uncertain.

"It bothered me enormously," he recalls. "I'm convinced some of my defeats trace back to that disadvantage. Once my vision was corrected, everything changed. The psychological edge alone was massive."

Baruto's experience is far from unique among elite athletes. When your sport demands split-second decisions, perfect spatial awareness, and unflinching confidence—clear vision isn't a luxury. It's essential.
