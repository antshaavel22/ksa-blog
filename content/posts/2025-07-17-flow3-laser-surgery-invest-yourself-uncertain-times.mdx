## The Lipstick Effect: Why We Invest in Ourselves When Money Gets Tight

Economists call it the **lipstick effect**. The logic is simple: when large purchases disappear—a new car, home renovation, holidays—people redirect spending toward smaller, more meaningful improvements. Not frivolous purchases, but things that genuinely improve daily life.

Researchers at UC Riverside confirmed this pattern: during economic downturns, spending on self-care and personal wellbeing actually *increases*. People swap expensive items for more affordable but impactful ones. It's not retail therapy—it's a conscious decision: **invest in yourself, not in things**. And this pattern repeats with every economic cycle as people learn to distinguish what truly matters.
