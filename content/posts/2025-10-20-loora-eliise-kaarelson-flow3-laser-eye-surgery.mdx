## When Your Vision Holds You Back

Loora-Eliise Kaarelson is an accomplished Estonian singer and performer. She's appeared in the TV series *Reetur*, the film *Truth and Justice*, and captured audiences nationwide on *Estonia's Got Talent*. Every week, she performs across Estonia with her musical duo, Loora & Kauro.

Behind every performance, every appearance, one thing had been getting in the way: myopia.

For more than half her life, Loora-Eliise wore glasses or contact lenses. With a prescription of -5 dioptres, vision correction wasn't optional—it was constant. And constant contact lens wear, especially during intensive performance schedules, takes its toll.

The problem wasn't just inconvenience. By the end of demanding touring seasons, her eyes would dry out. They'd feel tired. The lenses that once felt so practical had become a burden.

## The Breaking Point

One winter day, caught between the cold outside and warm indoors, Loora-Eliise's glasses frames simply snapped in half. Two weeks in contact lenses while waiting for replacements. The incident, absurd as it was, made her think: there had to be a better way.

She'd been dreaming about laser eye surgery for years, but her prescription kept shifting. It wasn't stable enough.

Then, during a routine eye exam at KSA Silmakeskus in spring, the news changed: her vision had finally stabilised. She could have the procedure done immediately.

The decision was simple. One summer of clear vision—no glasses, no lenses, no compromise. "Will I actually be able to swim this summer and see clearly?" she remembers thinking.

## Recovery Was Faster Than Expected

She'd prepared for two weeks of downtime. But her recovery surprised her.

By day four, she felt great. By day eight, she was back to her normal routine—performing, socialising, living fully. The world looked sharper than she remembered.

"Have you all always seen this clearly?" she asked friends, laughing. The clarity was almost disorienting.

## Why Flow3 Changed Everything

What made her recovery so smooth? [The Flow3 procedure](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) is different from traditional laser surgery. There's no flap cut into the cornea—only precise surface reshaping. That means less stress on the eye, faster healing, and safer outcomes for active people like her.

For a performer who travels constantly, who needs her eyes to work perfectly on stage and in daily life, this matters enormously. Stability returns in roughly one week instead of several months. There's no compromised corneal structure. There's no vulnerability to eye injury from sports or accidents.

The entire clinical team at KSA Silmakeskus—including our founder and lead surgeon Dr. Ants Haavel—chose [Flow3 for their own eyes](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/). When the specialists performing the surgery trust it enough for their own vision, that speaks louder than any marketing claim.

## Life Without Lenses

Today, Loora-Eliise performs without thinking about her vision. No lenses drying out mid-show. No glasses slipping or fogging. No emergency contact lens supplies backstage. Just clear sight and the freedom to focus on what matters: her music and her audience.

She's one of over 55,000 people who've chosen laser eye surgery at KSA Silmakeskus in Tallinn. And like many, she'd call it one of the best decisions she's ever made.
