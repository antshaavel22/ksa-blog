## Would you trust a surgeon who doesn't use their own treatment?

It's a fair question. If something is truly as good as promised, why wouldn't the person recommending it use it themselves?

When Ave Jõgibert joined KSA Silmakeskus as an optometrist, she was still wearing glasses. But she knows the [Flow3 procedure](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) inside out—and she trusts it completely. So in May 2017, she decided to experience it herself.

Now, she doesn't wear glasses at all.

## The difference between knowing and feeling

People with naturally clear vision often don't realise what they're missing. Sharp vision—something most take for granted—is a genuine gift when you've lived without it. Modern laser technology has evolved to the point where we no longer need to cut a flap into your cornea. [Flow3](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/) is flapless, safer, and delivers excellent results almost immediately.

Ave has been explaining this to patients for years. But living through it herself was different.

"I'm very happy," she says simply. "Life without glasses or contact lenses is so much freer. I don't have to worry about carrying spare lenses or backups when I'm away from home or heading to the gym after work."

## You do this once in your life

Yes, there was nervousness on procedure day. That's natural.

"This is something you only do once, and you can't really rehearse it," Ave explains. "A few days afterwards, my eyes felt uncomfortable and itchy—but I'd prepared myself for that. By day four, it was wonderful to wake up without discomfort. My vision got sharper every single day.

What struck me most was how quickly everything came back together. Within a week of the [procedure](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/), I could do everything I wanted. No restrictions at all."

## The real freedom: travel and sport

For someone who loves to travel and explore, glasses-free life is genuinely transformative.

Ave discovered surfing during a three-month student exchange in Hawaii. She worked as a sales assistant but spent every free moment in the water—surfing, snorkelling, swimming. Activities like these were frustrating with glasses. Contact lenses in salt water? Uncomfortable and risky.

"Wearing glasses during training or any sport was awkward," she says. "Swimming, water sports, even just going to the pool—it was nearly impossible before. Now I can do all of it, as much as I want."

Water sports have always drawn her. During school, she swam competitively in pools and open water. That freedom, that ease in the water—it's finally hers again, permanently.

And that's the thing about [Flow3](https://ksa.ee/en/laser-eye-surgery/): it's not just better vision. It's the life you get to live because of it.
