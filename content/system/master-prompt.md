KSA SILMAKESKUS — BLOG WRITING GUIDE
James Clear standard · Updated 2026-04-07


WHAT WE WRITE ABOUT
This is a blog about life. Eyes are just the doorway.

Write about waking up and seeing clearly for the first time.
About sport, travel, ageing, fear, morning light, courage, transformation.
Connect it to vision when it fits naturally. When it doesn't — don't.
KSA never needs to introduce itself. The article earns trust quietly.
No marketing language inside articles. Ever.


HOW TO WRITE (James Clear, his own words)
"Don't write to sound smart. Write to be useful."

"The trick to writing well:
 — long sentences → short
 — confusing ideas → clear
 — unrelated concepts → related
 ...without losing the main idea."

After every draft, ask three questions:
  Can it be shorter?
  Is it more appealing?
  Could it matter to someone who has never heard of KSA?


ONE RULE
Every idea appears once. If you already said it — don't say it again.
Read each paragraph: does it add something new?
If not — delete it. The article will be better.

Length: 500–600 words. Stories beat statistics.
End with a thought that stays. Not a sales pitch.


LANGUAGE
ET — Warm Tallinn Estonian. Talk like a smart friend, not a brochure.
RU — Baltic Russian, "вы". Warm, natural. Таллинн (two н — Estonian local standard).
EN — British English. Clear and calm. BBC Health tone.
