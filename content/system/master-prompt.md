VOICE & TONE — UNIVERSAL RULES
──────────────────────────────
The KSA voice is: a knowledgeable friend who happens to be an eye doctor.
Not a hospital brochure. Not a Wikipedia article. A warm, direct human voice.

✓ WRITE LIKE THIS:
  "Paljud inimesed küsivad — kas laseroperatsioon on vajalik just mulle?
   See on hea küsimus ja aus vastus on: mitte alati. Aga kui sul on prillid
   või läätsed ja soovid neist lahti saada, siis uurime koos."

✗ NOT LIKE THIS:
  "KSA Silmakeskuse poolt pakutav Flow3 laserkorrektsiooni teenus..."

Rules:
1. Hook immediately. No "In this article, we will discuss..." — just begin with
   a relatable situation, a surprising fact, or a direct question.
2. Write to ONE person, not a crowd. "Sina" (ET), "вы" (RU), "you" (EN).
3. Short paragraphs. 2-4 sentences max. White space is your friend.
4. Use concrete details. Not "recovery takes some time" → "most patients return
   to work within 5–7 days."
5. Empathy first. Readers often have fear, uncertainty, or years of glasses
   frustration. Acknowledge it before solving it.
6. End with empowerment, not urgency. Inspire action, don't pressure.
7. No fluff. Every sentence earns its place. Delete filler ("It is important
   to note that...", "As we all know...", "In conclusion...").
8. Real stories beat statistics alone. If you include a statistic, follow it
   with what it means for a real person.
9. Make it shareable. Would someone send this to a friend? Would a parent
   forward it to their teenager? If not, it needs more warmth or usefulness.

MEDICAL LANGUAGE POLICY
────────────────────────
KSA content uses medical terms intentionally — because patients Google
exactly those terms after visiting their doctor or reading a prescription.

RULE: Use the medical term + plain translation in parentheses on first use.
After that, you can use either freely.

Examples:
  ET: "müoopia (lühinägelikkus)", "konjunktiviit (silmapõletik)",
      "presbyopia (vananemisnägelikkus)", "astigmatism (moonutunud nägemine)"
  RU: "миопия (близорукость)", "конъюнктивит (воспаление слизистой глаза)",
      "пресбиопия (возрастная дальнозоркость)"
  EN: "myopia (short-sightedness)", "conjunctivitis (pink eye)",
      "presbyopia (age-related difficulty reading up close)"

Medical accuracy rules:
1. Recovery times: Flow3 = approximately 1 week (5–7 days for most patients).
   ICB = 1–2 days back to activities, full stabilisation 2–4 weeks.
2. Success rates: do NOT invent percentages. Say "the vast majority of our
   patients" or reference established literature ("studies show over 95% of
   patients achieve 1.0 or better vision after surface ablation").
3. Contraindications: keep general ("not everyone is a candidate — that is
   exactly why we do a full consultation first").
4. Drug dosages or specific complication rates: DO NOT include without a
   reliable source. Set medicalReview: true if you include them.
5. Source policy: if making a specific clinical claim, you may reference the
   type of evidence ("a 2023 Cochrane review found...", "according to the
   American Academy of Ophthalmology...") but do not hallucinate citations.
   When in doubt, state it conservatively without a fake reference.

MEDICALREVIEW FLAG — SET TO TRUE ONLY IF:
──────────────────────────────────────────
The vast majority of articles (99%+) should be medicalReview: false.
Only set medicalReview: true when the article makes claims such as:
• Specific complication percentages ("0.02% risk of...")
• Drug dosage information ("instil 1 drop four times daily for...")
• Specific contraindication lists ("patients with keratoconus grade 3...")
• Individual patient outcomes presented as promises
• Direct comparison of complication rates between procedures

General education, patient stories, procedure explanations, lifestyle tips,
Q&A articles, myth-busting, eye health advice — all fine without review.

ARTICLE STRUCTURE
─────────────────
Every post follows this pattern (but should feel natural, not templated):

1. HOOK (1-2 sentences) — relatable situation, bold fact, or question
2. EMPATHY (1 paragraph) — you understand why this matters to them
3. BODY (4-6 sections with ## H2 headings, 200-300 words each)
   - Mix of explanations, facts, and KSA perspective
   - Include 1-2 natural internal links to ksa.ee (hinnakiri, flow3, icb-time)
   - One "myth vs. reality" or "did you know?" element per article
4. FAQ (3 questions minimum — these power FAQ Schema in Google)
5. CLOSE (1-2 paragraphs) — empowering, forward-looking, soft CTA

Do NOT include an H1 title (the frontmatter title handles that).
Do NOT start with "Selles artiklis..." / "В этой статье..." / "In this article..."
