# Chapter 5 (Turvalisus) — research notes from AAO + Maus/sehkraft + competitor guides

## What AAO does WELL (we should adopt)

1. **Clear safety framing structure:**
   - Temporary side effects (vs)
   - Rare complications
   - Refractive outcomes
   - Long-term considerations

2. **Named risks honestly:**
   - Temporary: Dry eyes, changing vision, eye pain, hazy vision, scratchiness, glare,
     halos, double vision, decreased low-light vision, light sensitivity, blood patches
   - Rare: Eye infection, loss of best-corrected vision, blindness

3. **Exclusionary conditions list** — what makes you NOT a candidate:
   - Unstable refractive error
   - Extreme myopia/hyperopia/astigmatism
   - Severe dry eye
   - Thin corneas
   - Corneal scars
   - Keratoconus
   - Advanced glaucoma
   - Cataracts affecting vision
   - Certain eye infection history
   - Uncontrolled diabetes

4. **"90% achieve 20/20 to 20/40"** — single quotable success stat (though they don't
   cite source — KSA should cite source for our equivalent)

## What AAO does POORLY (we should improve)

1. **No specific complication percentages** — just lists "possible" risks
2. **No citations for the 90% stat**
3. **Limited long-term outcome data**
4. **Doesn't quantify patient expectations vs results**
5. **Doesn't differentiate between procedure families** (treats "LASIK" as monolithic)
6. **No "how safety is measured" discussion** (technology, screening, monitoring)

## What KSA's Chapter 5 should add beyond AAO

### A) Specific KSA data with internal-audit verification offer
- 55,000+ procedures
- 0 ectasia cases
- 7-year warranty performance: <3% retreatment rate
- Long-term follow-up commitment

### B) Procedure-family-specific risk breakdowns

| Risk | LASIK (flap) | Flow3 (surface) | SMILE |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ectasia (catastrophic) | Highest in modern era | Lowest | Vahepealne |
| Flap dislodgement | Yes — lifelong risk | None (no flap) | None (no flap) |
| Epithelial ingrowth | 1-3% | None | Rare |
| DLK ("Sands of Sahara") | Possible | None | None |
| Post-op dry eye persisting | Higher | Normalises 3-6mo | Less than LASIK |
| Suction loss intraop | n/a | n/a | 0.5% (1:200) |
| Lenticule tear | n/a | n/a | Rare |
| Halos/glare (>3mo) | 5-10% | 3-5% (SmartPulse advantage) | Similar to surface |

### C) "How safety is measured" — Reinstein-style technical chapter

This is where KSA can DIFFERENTIATE from AAO:
- Topography (Pentacam, Atlas) — what it measures, why critical
- Tomography — full corneal thickness map
- Pachymetry — minimum thickness rules (480μm for LASIK)
- Pupillometry — pupil size in dim light (>7mm = halo risk)
- Wavefront aberrometry — higher-order aberration mapping
- Tonometry — intraocular pressure
- Schirmer test + TBUT — dry eye screening
- Topography progression — repeat measurements over time

### D) "Red flags in clinic selection" — what KSA's competitors avoid discussing

- "Free consultation at the mall"
- Cookie-cutter screening
- Different surgeon at each visit
- No long-term follow-up plan
- Pricing only "on call"
- Same-day decision pressure
